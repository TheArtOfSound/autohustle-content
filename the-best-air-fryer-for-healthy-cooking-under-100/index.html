Buying Guide

Capacity Matters: Consider your household size when choosing capacity. A 2-3 quart model works for 1-2 people, while families need 4-6 quarts. Remember that you can't overfill air fryers – food needs space for proper air circulation.

Temperature Range and Controls: Look for models with wide temperature ranges (typically 180°F to 400°F) and precise controls. Digital displays with presets make cooking easier, while manual controls offer more flexibility for experienced cooks.

Build Quality and Materials: Check for non-stick, PFOA-free baskets that are dishwasher-safe. Sturdy construction with good warranties indicates better long-term value. Avoid models with flimsy plastic components that may break easily.

Additional Features: Consider what extras matter to you – preset programs for convenience, accessories for versatility, or app connectivity for guided cooking. However, don't pay for features you won't use.

FAQ

How much counter space do I need for an air fryer?

Most air fryers under $100 require 12-15 inches of counter space and at least 5 inches of clearance on all sides for proper ventilation.

Can I cook frozen foods directly in an air fryer?

Yes! Air fryers excel at cooking frozen foods like french fries, chicken nuggets, and vegetables without thawing. Just add 2-3 minutes to the recommended cooking time.

How often should I clean my air fryer?

Clean the basket and tray after each use, and wipe down the interior weekly. Most components are dishwasher-safe, making maintenance simple and quick.
