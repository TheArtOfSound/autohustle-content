Buying Guide

Color Accuracy and Coverage: Look for monitors with at least 95% sRGB coverage and ideally 90%+ DCI-P3 for modern video standards. Factory calibration or hardware calibration support ensures consistent, accurate colors across your workflow.

Resolution and Screen Size: 4K resolution provides the detail needed for modern video editing, while 27-32 inches offers the sweet spot between screen real estate and desk space. Larger screens help with timeline management and multi-window workflows.

Connectivity Options: USB-C connectivity with power delivery simplifies laptop setups, while multiple inputs allow easy switching between devices. Look for monitors with at least DisplayPort and HDMI inputs for maximum compatibility.

Panel Technology: IPS panels offer the best color accuracy and viewing angles for video editing, though some high-end VA panels can also work well. Avoid TN panels for professional video work due to poor color reproduction.

FAQ

What's the minimum color accuracy needed for video editing?

For professional work, aim for monitors covering at least 95% sRGB and 90% DCI-P3 color spaces with Delta E values under 2.

Is 4K resolution necessary for video editing?

While not absolutely necessary, 4K resolution provides valuable screen real estate and allows you to view 4K footage at native resolution for accurate detail assessment.

Should I buy a curved or flat monitor for video editing?

Flat monitors are generally preferred for video editing as they provide more accurate geometry representation, though personal preference plays a role for larger displays.
