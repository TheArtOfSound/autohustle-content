Buying Guide: What to Consider When Choosing a Standing Desk Under $300

Height Range and Adjustability: Look for desks that accommodate your height when both sitting and standing. Most people need a range of 28-46 inches. Electric adjustment is more convenient but manual options work well too. Consider whether you need memory presets if multiple people will use the desk.

Stability and Weight Capacity: A wobbly desk defeats the purpose of improving your workspace. Look for steel frames and weight capacities that exceed your equipment needs by at least 50 pounds. Wider leg spreads generally provide better stability, especially at maximum height.

Desktop Size and Shape: Consider your equipment needs â€“ multiple monitors require more width, while deep keyboards and documents need more depth. Standard sizes range from 24" deep by 48" wide to 30" deep by 60" wide. Rectangular shapes offer the most versatility.

Assembly and Installation: Some desks require extensive assembly while others come pre-built. Desktop converters typically require no assembly, while full desk replacements may take 1-2 hours to set up. Check reviews for assembly difficulty if you're not handy with tools.

FAQ

How much money should I spend on a standing desk?

For basic functionality, $200-300 provides excellent options with electric adjustment and solid build quality. Spending more gets you premium materials, longer warranties, and advanced features, but isn't necessary for most users.

Are cheap standing desks worth it?

Yes, budget standing desks under $300 can provide the same health benefits as expensive models. Focus on stability, smooth adjustment, and adequate weight capacity rather than premium materials or advanced features.

What's the difference between electric and manual standing desks?

Electric desks adjust at the push of a button and often include memory presets, while manual desks require hand-crank operation. Electric is more convenient for frequent adjustments, but manual options are more reliable and cost-effective.
