Buying Guide

Padding and Protection: Look for gloves with adequate foam or gel padding in the palm area where calluses typically form. The padding should be thick enough to prevent friction but not so bulky that it interferes with your grip on equipment.

Material Quality: Choose gloves made from durable materials like genuine leather or high-quality synthetic alternatives. The palm material should provide good grip while the back should be breathable to prevent excessive sweating.

Fit and Comfort: Proper sizing is crucial for effectiveness. Gloves should fit snugly without being too tight, allowing for natural hand movement while maintaining protection. Look for adjustable wrist closures for a customized fit.

Breathability: Mesh panels or moisture-wicking materials help prevent bacterial growth and keep hands comfortable during extended workout sessions. This feature is especially important if you train frequently or in warm environments.

FAQ

How do I know what size workout gloves to buy?

Measure the circumference of your hand around the knuckles (excluding the thumb) and compare it to the manufacturer's sizing chart. Most brands provide detailed measurements for accurate fitting.

Can I wash my workout gloves in the washing machine?

Many workout gloves are machine washable, but always check the manufacturer's care instructions. Air drying is typically recommended to preserve the materials and prevent shrinkage.

How often should I replace my workout gloves?

Replace gloves when the padding becomes compressed, the grip surface wears smooth, or you notice tears in high-stress areas. With regular use, quality gloves typically last 6-12 months.
