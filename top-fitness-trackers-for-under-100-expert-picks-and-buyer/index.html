Buying Guide: What to Consider

Battery Life and Charging Method

Look for trackers offering at least 5-7 days of battery life to minimize charging frequency. Consider whether the device uses proprietary charging cables or more convenient magnetic charging systems.

Health Monitoring Accuracy

Prioritize trackers with optical heart rate sensors and, if important to you, additional sensors for blood oxygen or stress monitoring. Read reviews specifically mentioning accuracy compared to medical-grade devices.

App Integration and Ecosystem

Consider which smartphone platform you use and whether the tracker's companion app offers the insights and social features you want. Some apps provide better coaching and goal-setting tools than others.

Build Quality and Comfort

Since you'll wear this device daily, ensure the band material won't cause skin irritation and the overall construction feels durable enough for your lifestyle and activity level.

FAQ

How accurate are fitness trackers under $100 compared to expensive models?

Modern budget fitness trackers are surprisingly accurate for basic metrics like steps and heart rate, typically within 5-10% of premium models. The main differences lie in advanced features and build quality rather than core tracking accuracy.

Can budget fitness trackers track swimming activities?

Most trackers under $100 offer water resistance suitable for swimming, but automatic swim stroke detection varies by model. Check the specific water resistance rating (5 ATM minimum for swimming) and available swim-specific features.

Do these trackers work with both iPhone and Android?

Yes, most fitness trackers in this price range support both iOS and Android, though some features may work better with specific platforms. Samsung devices integrate best with Android, while others maintain platform neutrality.
