Buying Guide

When shopping for a budget gaming monitor, there are several key factors to consider. Here are three things to keep in mind:

  1. Resolution: Look for monitors with high resolutions (Full HD or QHD) and high refresh rates (144Hz or higher).
  2. Response time: A fast response time is essential for reducing ghosting and blurring.
  3. G-Sync compatibility: If you have a NVIDIA graphics card, look for monitors that support G-Sync technology to reduce screen tearing.

FAQ

#### Q: What's the difference between G-Sync and FreeSync?

A: Both technologies are designed to eliminate screen tearing, but G-Sync is exclusive to NVIDIA graphics cards, while FreeSync is available on AMD graphics cards.

#### Q: Can I use HDR10 on my budget gaming monitor?

A: Yes, many budget gaming monitors support HDR10, which offers improved color accuracy and contrast. However, the quality of the display may vary depending on the specific model.

#### Q: Are budget gaming monitors worth it?

A: Absolutely! Budget gaming monitors offer excellent performance and features at a lower price point than high-end models. You can get a great gaming experience without breaking the bank.
