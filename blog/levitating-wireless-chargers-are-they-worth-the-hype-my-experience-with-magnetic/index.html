Levitating wireless chargers: Are they worth the hype? My experience with magnetic charging tech

Published: February 22, 2026

wireless_chargingmagnetic_levitationtech_reviewgadgets

Been testing levitating wireless chargers lately and wanted to share some insights:

Key findings:

• Magnetic levitation uses electromagnetic fields to suspend your phone while charging - pretty cool physics in action

• Charging speeds are typically 10W max (similar to standard wireless pads)

• The "wow factor" is real - guests always ask about it

Practical considerations:

• Works best in stable environments (vibrations can disrupt levitation)

• Phone needs to be perfectly aligned for both charging and floating

• More of a desk showpiece than everyday convenience tool

Bottom line: If you want something that sparks conversation and don't mind paying premium for the novelty, it's genuinely impressive tech. For pure functionality, regular wireless chargers are more reliable.

Anyone else tried these? Curious about long-term durability experiences.

TL;DR: Levitating chargers are cool tech that works as advertised, but prioritize novelty over practicality. Great conversation starter, decent charging performance.
