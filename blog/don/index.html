How to Prevent Wrist Pain from Push-Ups: Essential Tips for Fitness Enthusiasts

Push-ups are a cornerstone exercise for building upper body strength, but they shouldn't come at the cost of wrist pain. If you've ever finished a workout with aching wrists, you're not alone. Fortunately, with the right approach, you can maintain your push-up routine while keeping your wrists healthy and pain-free.

Master Your Hand Placement

The foundation of pain-free push-ups starts with proper hand positioning. Place your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, with fingers spread wide and pointing forward. This distributes weight evenly across your palms and reduces pressure on your wrists. Avoid turning your hands outward, as this creates unnecessary strain on the joint.

Strengthen Your Wrists Gradually

Many fitness enthusiasts jump into high-volume push-up routines without adequate wrist preparation. Start by incorporating wrist mobility exercises into your warm-up routine. Simple wrist circles, flexor stretches, and prayer stretches can significantly improve flexibility and strength. Consider beginning with modified push-ups on your knees or against a wall to build wrist endurance progressively.

Explore Alternative Hand Positions

If traditional push-ups continue to cause discomfort, experiment with push-up handles, dumbbells, or fist push-ups. These variations maintain a neutral wrist position, eliminating the extreme extension that often triggers pain. Push-up handles are particularly effective as they allow your wrists to stay in a more natural alignment while still providing an excellent workout.

Listen to Your Body
