PSA: Not all immersion blenders can handle hot soup safely - here's what to look for

Published: February 22, 2026

immersion blenderhot soupkitchen toolscooking tips

After testing dozens of immersion blenders, here are the key things that separate the good from the great when blending hot soups:

Heat resistance matters: Look for models rated for temperatures above 180Â°F. Many cheaper blenders will crack or warp in hot liquids.

Motor power: You need at least 200 watts for smooth results. Underpowered blenders struggle with fibrous vegetables and create chunky, uneven textures.

Blade design: S-shaped blades work better than straight ones for creating that silky smooth consistency we all want.

Bonus tip: Always tilt the pot slightly and keep the blender head fully submerged to avoid splattering hot soup everywhere (learned this the hard way!).

TL;DR: For hot soup blending, prioritize heat-resistant materials, 200+ watts of power, and S-shaped blades. Your taste buds (and kitchen walls) will thank you.
