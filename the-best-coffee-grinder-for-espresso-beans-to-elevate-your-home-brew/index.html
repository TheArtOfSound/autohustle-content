Buying Guide

Grind Consistency: Look for burr grinders rather than blade models, as burrs produce uniform particle sizes essential for proper espresso extraction. Conical burrs excel at low speeds with minimal heat generation, while flat burrs offer exceptional uniformity.

Adjustability: Espresso requires fine-tuning, so seek grinders with micro-adjustment capabilities. Stepless adjustment systems provide infinite control, while stepped systems should offer at least 40+ settings in the espresso range.

Retention: Choose grinders with minimal retention to ensure maximum freshness and reduce waste. Single-dose grinders or those with straight-through designs minimize leftover grounds between sessions.

Build Quality: Invest in solid construction with quality burrs that will maintain their sharpness over time. Commercial-grade components ensure longevity and consistent performance through thousands of grinding sessions.

FAQ

How fine should I grind espresso beans?

Espresso requires a fine grind similar to powdered sugar, but not quite as fine as flour. The exact setting depends on your machine, beans, and desired extraction time—typically 25-30 seconds for a double shot.

Can I use a blade grinder for espresso?

While possible, blade grinders produce inconsistent particle sizes that lead to uneven extraction and poor-tasting espresso. Burr grinders are essential for quality espresso preparation.

How often should I clean my espresso grinder?

Clean your grinder weekly with regular use, removing oils and residue that can turn rancid. Deep clean monthly by running grinder cleaning tablets through the system according to manufacturer instructions.
