4. Risk Factors

4.1 Market Volatility & Liquidations

  • Sudden price swings can trigger margin calls and liquidations, exacerbating downturns.
  • Leveraged trading remains a risk, especially in bearish conditions.

4.2 Regulatory Uncertainty

  • U.S. SEC crackdowns on exchanges and stablecoins.
  • EU MiCA regulations may impact crypto operations.
  • China’s crypto ban enforcement remains a long-term risk.

4.3 Macro Economic Pressures

  • Rising interest rates reduce risk appetite for crypto.
  • Inflation fears may lead to capital flight from crypto to gold or bonds.

4.4 Security Risks

  • Exchange hacks and smart contract exploits remain a threat.
  • DeFi vulnerabilities (e.g., flash loan attacks) could trigger further sell-offs.

5.3 Long-Term (6-12 Months)

  • Bitcoin as a hedge against inflation may regain traction.
  • DeFi and Web3 growth could drive altcoin recovery.
  • Regulatory clarity (if positive) may boost institutional participation.

5.4 Investment Opportunities

  • Bitcoin (BTC) – Long-term hold for macro hedge.
