Outward Hound Brick Puzzle - Is It Worth It?

Pet owners everywhere are discovering what trainers have known for years: a tired mind is often more important than tired paws. The Outward Hound Brick Puzzle has become a go-to solution for dogs and cats who turn destructive when bored, offering mental stimulation that can transform your pet's day.

What makes this puzzle stand out:

• Adjustable difficulty levels - You can make it easier for beginners by leaving some compartments open, or create maximum challenge by using all the sliding pieces and removable bones

• Sturdy construction - Unlike flimsy puzzle toys that break after a few uses, this brick design holds up to determined paws and teeth

• Multiple hiding spots - With various compartments, sliding pieces, and removable elements, it keeps pets engaged longer than single-solution puzzles

• Easy to clean - All pieces come apart for thorough washing, which you'll appreciate after your pet drools all over it

Who should buy this:

This puzzle works best for food-motivated dogs and cats who already show interest in problem-solving. If your pet enjoys treat-dispensing toys or naturally paws at things to investigate, they'll likely love this challenge. It's particularly great for pets who eat too quickly or need mental stimulation while you're working from home.

Who shouldn't buy this:

Skip this if your pet destroys toys within minutes or shows no interest in food puzzles. Very large dogs might find the compartments too small for their paws, while extremely anxious pets might get frustrated rather than engaged.

Real pros and cons:

Pros:

  • Genuinely slows down fast eaters
  • Keeps most pets occupied for 10-20 minutes
  • Dishwasher safe (top rack)
  • Good value compared to electronic puzzle toys
  • Works for both dogs and cats

Cons:

  • Some pets solve it too quickly after the first few tries
  • Sliding pieces can get lost if you're not careful
  • Not suitable for heavy chewers who might break the plastic
  • Can be noisy on hard floors when pets push it around

Price and value assessment:

At $10-20, this puzzle offers solid value for pet enrichment. Compare that to a single training session ($50-100) or replacing chewed furniture, and it's a bargain. The durability means it should last months or even years with normal use. However, don't expect it to replace daily exercise or training - it's a supplement, not a solution.

The puzzle typically pays for itself if it prevents even one instance of destructive behavior or provides regular mental stimulation for a bored pet.
