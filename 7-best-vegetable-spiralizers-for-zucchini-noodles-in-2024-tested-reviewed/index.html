Buying Guide

Blade Quality: Look for sharp, stainless steel blades that stay sharp over time. Dull blades create mushy noodles and require more pressure, making the tool harder to use.

Stability: A good spiralizer should stay put during use. Countertop models need strong suction cups or a heavy base, while handheld versions require comfortable, non-slip grips.

Ease of Cleaning: Zucchini can be pulpy, so choose models that disassemble easily and are dishwasher safe. Built-in storage for blades is a bonus.

Versatility: Consider how many vegetables you'll spiralize. Multiple blade options let you create different noodle thicknesses and work with various vegetable sizes.

FAQ

How do I prevent zucchini noodles from getting watery?

Salt your zoodles lightly and let them sit for 10 minutes, then pat dry with paper towels before cooking. This removes excess moisture.

Can I use these spiralizers on other vegetables?

Yes! Most work great with carrots, sweet potatoes, cucumbers, and other firm vegetables. Avoid very soft vegetables that might break apart.

How long do spiralized zucchini noodles last?

Fresh zoodles keep in the refrigerator for 2-3 days when stored in an airtight container with paper towels to absorb moisture.
