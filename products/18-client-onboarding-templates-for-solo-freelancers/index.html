Template 3: Freelance Contract Outline (Simple Version)

When to use: Before starting work, to formalize scope, payment, and deliverables

Freelance Agreement

This agreement is between [Your Full Name] (“Freelancer”) and [Client Full Name or Company] (“Client”) as of [Date].

1. Project Scope

The Freelancer will provide the following services:

[Detailed description of deliverables, e.g., “Design a responsive 5-page website using Figma”]

2. Project Timeline

  • Start Date: [Start Date]
  • Delivery Date: [Delivery Date]
  • Revisions: [Number] rounds of revisions included

3. Payment Terms

  • Total Fee: [Amount]
  • Deposit: [Amount or %] due upon signing
  • Final Payment: [Amount or %] due upon delivery
  • Payment Method: [e.g., Bank Transfer, PayPal, Wise]
  • Late Payments: A fee of [X]% per month will be applied to overdue balances

4. Ownership & Usage

Upon full payment, final deliverables will be provided, and [Client Name] will own the rights to use them for [intended use]. Source files available upon request for an additional fee.

5. Cancellation

  • If Client cancels within [X] days of start: Deposit is non-refundable
  • If Freelancer cancels: Full refund of any payments made

6. Communication

All project communication will occur via [Preferred Channel, e.g., email or Slack]. Response time: within [X] business days.

By replying “I agree,” you accept the terms of this contract.

Sincerely,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Business Name]

[Email Address]

Template 6: Client Onboarding Call Agenda

Project: [Project Name]

Date: [Call Date]

Time: [Call Time] | Duration: [e.g., 30 minutes]

Host: [Your Name]

  • Brief personal intro
  • Thank client for partnering with you

Project Overview (10 min)

  • Recap goal and deliverables
  • Confirm timeline and milestones

Client Goals & Success Metrics (10 min)

  • “What does success look like for you?”
  • Discuss KPIs or desired outcomes

Communication & Workflow (5 min)

  • Preferred contact method
  • Feedback turnaround expectations
  • Tools we’ll use (e.g., email, Trello, Google Drive)

Q&A (5 min)

  • Address client questions
  • Clarify any concerns

Next Steps

  • Confirm when first draft will arrive
  • Share any immediate action items

Note: This call will not be recorded unless agreed in advance.

Template 11: Client Questionnaire

When to use: To gather essential information from the client about their project requirements, preferences, and expectations.

Client Questionnaire for [PROJECT NAME]

Dear [CLIENT NAME],

To ensure we deliver a project that meets your expectations, we have prepared this questionnaire to gather more information about your needs and preferences. Please take a few minutes to fill out the following questions:

  1. What are your primary goals for this project?
  2. Who is your target audience?
  3. What sets your project apart from others in the same industry?
  4. Do you have any specific design or content preferences (e.g., colors, fonts, tone)?
  5. Are there any existing brand guidelines (e.g., logos, style guides) we should follow?
  6. What is your expected timeline for the project?
  7. How do you envision our working relationship, and what kind of communication do you prefer?
  8. Are there any specific metrics or outcomes you will use to measure the success of this project?
  9. Do you have any existing materials (e.g., images, videos, documents) that should be incorporated into the project?

10. Is there anything else you think we should know about your project or expectations?

Thank you for taking the time to provide this information. Your input is invaluable to us, and we look forward to reviewing your responses.

Best regards,

[FREELANCER NAME]
