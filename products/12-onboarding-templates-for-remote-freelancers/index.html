12 Onboarding Templates for Remote Freelancers

Updated: March 07, 2026

12 onboarding templates for remote freelancers — streamline client setup with professional emails, forms, and workflow guides.

What's Inside

  • 12 plug-and-play templates
  • covers welcome emails, intake forms, and project kickoffs
  • designed for remote collaboration

Template 1: Client Welcome Email

When to use: Immediately after a client agrees to work with you — sets a professional tone and confirms next steps.

Hi [Client Name],

Welcome! I’m so excited to be working with you on [Project Name]. I’ve been looking forward to helping you [brief goal, e.g., launch your new website, streamline your content calendar, etc.].

To get started, I’ve put together a quick onboarding process to make everything smooth and organized. Over the next few days, you’ll receive:

  • A project agreement outlining scope, timeline, and deliverables
  • A brief intake form to gather key details
  • A scheduling link to book our kickoff call

If you have any immediate questions or need to share files or inspiration, feel free to reply to this email.

Looking forward to building something great together!

Best regards,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Job Title, e.g., Freelance Web Designer]

[Your Website URL]

[Your Phone Number, optional]

Template 2: Project Intake Form

When to use: After the welcome email — collect essential project details from the client.

Project Intake Form

Hi [Client Name],

To help me understand your vision and set this project up for success, please fill out the details below. Your answers will guide our collaboration and ensure I deliver exactly what you need.

1. Project Overview

  • Project Name: [Project Name]
  • Goal of the project: [e.g., Increase conversions, relaunch brand, grow email list]
  • Target audience: [e.g., Busy professionals aged 30–45, eco-conscious parents]

2. Deliverables

  • What are you expecting from this project? (e.g., 5 web pages, 8 blog posts, logo + brand kit)

[Deliverables List]

3. Timeline

  • Desired launch/completion date: [Date]
  • Any key milestones or deadlines? [List if applicable]

4. Brand Details (if applicable)

  • Brand colors: [HEX codes or names]
  • Fonts: [Font names]
  • Brand voice: [e.g., Professional, friendly, bold, minimalist]
  • Link to brand guidelines: [URL or “N/A”]

5. Inspiration & References

  • Websites, designs, or content you like: [URLs or descriptions]
  • Examples of what you want to avoid: [URLs or descriptions]

6. Stakeholders

  • Who will provide feedback/approvals? [Name and role]
  • Any specific communication preferences for them? [e.g., CC on emails, only one point of contact]

7. Additional Notes

  • Anything else I should know before we begin?

[Open text field]

Please reply to this email with your completed form by [Due Date]. Let me know if you’d prefer a Google Form version!

Thanks,

[Your Name]

Template 3: Project Kickoff Call Agenda

When to use: Before your first project meeting — send this to align expectations and keep the call focused.

Hi [Client Name],

I’m looking forward to our project kickoff call on [Date] at [Time, including time zone]. This meeting will help us align on goals, timelines, and communication for [Project Name].

Call Agenda (30–45 minutes):

  1. Introductions & Goals (5 min)
  • Brief personal intro
  • Confirm the main objective of the project
  1. Project Scope & Deliverables (10 min)
  • Review agreed-upon deliverables
  • Clarify any uncertainties
  1. Timeline & Milestones (5 min)
  • Confirm key dates and deadlines
  • Discuss flexibility, if needed
  1. Communication Plan (5 min)
  • Best ways to reach each other
  • Frequency of updates and check-ins
  1. Feedback & Revisions (5 min)
  • How you’d like to provide feedback (email, comments, calls)
  • Number of revision rounds included
  1. Next Steps (5 min)
  • Confirm action items and who owns them
  • Share project management tool access, if applicable

If you have additional topics to discuss, please let me know in advance so I can adjust the agenda.

See you soon!

[Your Name]

Template 4: Communication Guidelines Document

When to use: After the kickoff call — set clear expectations for how you’ll communicate during the project.

Hi [Client Name],

Thanks for a great kickoff call! To keep our collaboration clear and efficient, here are our agreed-upon communication guidelines for [Project Name].

Preferred Channels:

  • General updates & questions: [e.g., Email or Slack]
  • File sharing: [e.g., Google Drive or Dropbox]
  • Urgent issues (rare): [e.g., Phone call or text — only if pre-agreed]
  • Project updates: [e.g., Weekly status reports via email]

Response Times:

  • I aim to respond to messages within [e.g., 24 business hours]
  • I work [e.g., Monday–Friday, 9 AM–5 PM in your time zone]
  • Outside those hours, I’ll respond on the next business day

Feedback Process:

  • Please share feedback in [e.g., written comments on Google Docs]
  • Consolidate feedback from all stakeholders before sending
  • I include [Number] rounds of revisions per deliverable

Meeting Schedule:

  • Weekly check-in: [Day, Time, Time Zone] via [Zoom/Google Meet]
  • Cancel or reschedule at least [e.g., 24 hours] in advance

Let me know if anything needs clarification. These guidelines help us stay aligned and avoid miscommunication.

Best,

[Your Name]

Template 5: Freelance Contract Template

When to use: Before work begins — formalize the agreement with scope, payment, and terms.

Freelance Service Agreement

Client: [Client Full Name or Company Name]

Freelancer: [Your Full Name]

Project: [Project Name]

Effective Date: [Date]

1. Scope of Work

The Freelancer agrees to provide the following services:

  • [Deliverable 1, e.g., Design 5 responsive web pages]
  • [Deliverable 2, e.g., Create brand style guide]
  • [Deliverable 3, e.g., Deliver final files in Figma and PDF]

Any work outside this scope requires a written change order and additional fees.

2. Project Timeline

  • Project start date: [Date]
  • Estimated completion date: [Date]
  • Key milestones:
  • [Milestone 1, e.g., Draft design – Due Date]
  • [Milestone 2, e.g., Final revisions – Due Date]

3. Payment Terms

  • Total project fee: [Amount, e.g., $2,500]
  • Payment schedule:
  • [e.g., 50% upfront: $1,250 due on signing]
  • [e.g., 50% upon delivery: $1,250 due by Final Date]
  • Payment method: [e.g., Bank transfer, PayPal, Wise]
  • Late payments: Subject to [e.g., 1.5% monthly interest]

4. Revisions

  • Includes [Number] rounds of revisions per deliverable
  • Additional revisions: [e.g., $75/hour or flat rate $100]

5. Ownership & Rights

  • Upon final payment, full rights to deliverables transfer to the Client
  • Freelancer retains the right to display work in portfolio unless otherwise agreed

6. Termination

  • Either party may terminate with [e.g., 7 days] written notice
  • Client pays for all completed work up to termination date

7. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to keep project details and business information confidential.

Signatures

Client Signature: _________________________ Date: _________

Name: [Client Full Name]

Freelancer Signature: _____________________ Date: _________

Name: [Your Full Name]

Template 7: Remote Freelance Contract Agreement

When to use: When finalizing the terms of a project with a new client to ensure both parties are clear on expectations and responsibilities.

This Remote Freelance Contract Agreement ("Agreement") is made on [DATE] by and between [FREELANCER NAME] ([FREELANCER COMPANY], if applicable) with a principal place of business at [ADDRESS] ("Freelancer") and [CLIENT NAME] with a principal place of business at [CLIENT ADDRESS] ("Client").

  1. Scope of Work: The Freelancer agrees to perform the following services for the Client: [DESCRIPTION OF SERVICES].
  2. Payment Terms: The Client shall pay the Freelancer as follows: [PAYMENT TERMS, INCLUDING AMOUNTS AND SCHEDULE].
  3. Intellectual Property: The Freelancer retains ownership of all intellectual property rights in and to the work produced under this Agreement, except as explicitly agreed otherwise in writing by the parties.
  4. Confidentiality: Both parties agree to keep confidential all information exchanged in the course of this project.
  5. Termination: This Agreement may be terminated by either party upon [NUMBER] days' written notice to the other.

By signing below, the parties acknowledge that they have read, understand, and agree to be bound by the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

Signature of Freelancer: ______________________ Date: ______________________

Signature of Client: ______________________ Date: ______________________

Template 10: Project Kickoff Meeting Agenda

When to use: To structure and guide the initial meeting with the client to ensure all key topics are covered.

Project Kickoff Meeting Agenda for [PROJECT NAME]

  1. Introduction and Icebreaker (5 minutes):
  • Brief introductions of all attendees.
  • Overview of the project's objectives and scope.
  1. Project Overview and Expectations (15 minutes):
  • Detailed review of the project scope, timeline, and milestones.
  • Discussion of client expectations and goals.
  1. Communication Plan (10 minutes):
  • Preferred communication channels and frequency.
  • How to handle questions, concerns, and feedback.
  1. Project Timeline and Key Dates (15 minutes):
  • Review of the project schedule, including deadlines and milestones.
  • Discussion of any dependencies or critical path activities.
  1. Next Steps and Action Items (10 minutes):
  • Summary of tasks and responsibilities for both the freelancer and the client.
  • Establishment of the next meeting or check-in.
  1. Q&A and Conclusion (5 minutes):
  • Open discussion for any questions or concerns.
  • Final thoughts and closing remarks.

Meeting Details:

  • Date: [DATE]
  • Time: [TIME]
  • Location/Platform: [LOCATION OR PLATFORM]

Please come prepared with any questions or topics you would like to discuss.

Best regards,

[FREELANCER NAME]
