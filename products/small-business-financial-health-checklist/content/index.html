Small Business Financial Health Checklist

Financial Health Check

Check your financial health by reviewing these key metrics:

  • Current Accounts:
  • [ ] Cash Reserves (3-6 months)
  • [ ] Accounts Receivable (Days Sales Outstanding)
  • [ ] Inventory Turns
  • Current Liabilities:
  • [ ] Accounts Payable (Days Sales Outstanding)
  • [ ] Short-term Debt (e.g. credit cards, loans)
  • Operating Expenses:
  • [ ] Salaries and Wages (as a percentage of revenue)
  • [ ] Marketing and Advertising Expenses
  • [ ] Rent or Mortgage Payments
  • Cash Flow:
  • [ ] Current Cash Balance
  • [ ] Projected Cash Flows (6-12 months)

Review Your Numbers

Take an in-depth look at your financial data to identify trends, areas for improvement, and opportunities for growth:

  • Analyze your income statement to understand revenue streams and expense categories.
  • Review your balance sheet to assess your asset allocation and liquidity position.
  • Examine your cash flow statement to track cash inflows and outflows.

Identify Areas for Improvement

Prioritize areas that need attention to ensure the long-term health of your business:

  • High-Risk Areas:
  • [ ] Cash Flow Management (insufficient or irregular cash flows)
  • [ ] Accounts Receivable Aging (delinquent payments or slow collections)
  • [ ] Inventory Turns (slow or inefficient inventory management)
  • Medium-Term Improvements:
  • [ ] Staffing and Training ( inadequate workforce or training programs)
  • [ ] Marketing and Advertising Expenses (uneffective or costly campaigns)
  • [ ] Rent or Mortgage Payments (unsustainable or high costs)

Create a Plan to Improve

Develop a comprehensive plan to address identified areas for improvement:

  • Short-Term Goals:
  • [ ] Implement new financial management processes
  • [ ] Adjust staffing and training programs
  • [ ] Review and optimize marketing and advertising expenses
  • Medium-Term Objectives:
  • [ ] Increase cash flow through efficient billing and collections
  • [ ] Improve accounts receivable aging
  • [ ] Optimize inventory turns
  • Long-Term Vision:
  • [ ] Develop a sustainable financial model
  • [ ] Enhance staff skills and knowledge
  • [ ] Launch targeted marketing campaigns

Action Items and Next Steps

Implement the plan outlined in this checklist to improve your small business's financial health:

1. Review and analyze your financial data.

2. Identify high-risk areas for improvement.

3. Develop a comprehensive plan to address identified issues.

4. Implement short-term goals.

5. Monitor progress and adjust as needed.

By following this checklist, you'll be able to assess your small business's financial health, identify areas for improvement, and create a plan to drive growth and sustainability in the long term.
