12 High-Converting Email Templates for Freelance Designers

Updated: March 07, 2026

12 high-converting email templates for freelance designers — covers outreach, onboarding, revisions, and follow-ups.

What's Inside

  • 12 proven email templates
  • tailored for freelance design workflows
  • easy to customize for any niche

Template 1: Introduction and Services Offer

When to use: When reaching out to potential clients to introduce your services and inquire about their design needs.

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

I came across [CLIENT COMPANY] and was impressed with the great work you're doing in [INDUSTRY]. As a freelance designer with [NUMBER] years of experience, I specialize in creating visually stunning and effective designs that drive results.

My services include [LIST OF SERVICES, e.g., logo design, branding, web design, etc.]. I'd love to learn more about your current design needs and see if I can help.

Please let me know if you're open to discussing further. I've included a link to my portfolio for your review: [PORTFOLIO LINK].

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 2: Proposal and Quote

When to use: After discussing a project with a potential client and you're ready to submit a formal proposal and quote.

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

I hope this email finds you well. As discussed, I've put together a proposal for the [PROJECT NAME] project. Below, you'll find the details of the scope, timeline, and quote for the work.

Project Scope:

[LIST OF SPECIFIC SERVICES TO BE PROVIDED]

Timeline:

[PROJECT TIMELINE, INCLUDING KEY MILESTONES]

Quote:

The total quote for the project is [QUOTE AMOUNT]. This includes [LIST OF WHAT'S INCLUDED IN THE QUOTE].

Please let me know if you have any questions or if you're ready to move forward. I've attached the full proposal document for your review: [PROPOSAL DOCUMENT ATTACHMENT].

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 3: Onboarding and Welcome

When to use: After a client has agreed to work with you and you're starting the onboarding process.

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

A warm welcome to [YOUR COMPANY NAME]! I'm excited to start working on your [PROJECT NAME] project. As discussed, I've outlined the next steps below to ensure a smooth onboarding process.

Next Steps:

  1. [FIRST ACTION ITEM, e.g., scheduling a call]
  2. [SECOND ACTION ITEM, e.g., sharing necessary files]
  3. [THIRD ACTION ITEM, e.g., setting up project management tools]

If you have any questions or concerns, please don't hesitate to reach out. I'm looking forward to working together to bring your vision to life.

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 4: Request for Project Information

When to use: When you need more information from the client to proceed with the project.

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

I hope you're doing well. I'm making great progress on your [PROJECT NAME] project, but I need a bit more information to ensure everything is to your liking.

Could you please provide the following:

  • [SPECIFIC INFORMATION NEEDED, e.g., brand guidelines]
  • [ADDITIONAL INFORMATION, e.g., specific design preferences]
  • [ANY OTHER DETAILS REQUIRED]

You can share this information via [PREFERRED METHOD, e.g., email, shared drive]. I appreciate your prompt response, as this will help me stay on schedule.

Thank you for your time, and I look forward to hearing back from you.

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 5: Design Concept Presentation

When to use: When you're ready to present the initial design concepts to the client.

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

I'm excited to share the initial design concepts for your [PROJECT NAME] project. As discussed, I've developed [NUMBER] unique concepts that aim to capture the essence of your brand and meet your project goals.

You can view the design concepts here: [LINK TO DESIGN CONCEPTS]. I've also included a brief overview of each concept to help guide your review.

Please let me know your thoughts on these concepts. Your feedback is invaluable in helping me refine the design to perfectly suit your needs.

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 6: Revision Request

When to use: When the client requests revisions to the design work.

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

Thank you for your feedback on the design concepts. I'm happy to make revisions to ensure the final product meets your expectations.

To confirm, you'd like me to [LIST SPECIFIC REVISIONS REQUESTED]. I've outlined the next steps and timeline for these revisions below:

Revision Plan:

  • [FIRST REVISION STEP]
  • [SECOND REVISION STEP]
  • [EXPECTED COMPLETION DATE]

Please let me know if this plan aligns with your expectations. I'm committed to delivering high-quality work that you'll love.

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 7: Project Completion and Delivery

When to use: When the project is complete and you're delivering the final design work to the client.

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

I'm pleased to announce that your [PROJECT NAME] project is now complete! I've attached the final design files for your review: [FINAL DESIGN FILES ATTACHMENT].

Next Steps:

  1. [FIRST ACTION ITEM, e.g., reviewing the files]
  2. [SECOND ACTION ITEM, e.g., providing final payment]
  3. [ANY ADDITIONAL STEPS]

If you have any questions or need any assistance, don't hesitate to reach out. I'm grateful for the opportunity to work with you and hope you're satisfied with the final outcome.

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 8: Follow-Up and Feedback Request

When to use: After completing a project and you want to follow up with the client for feedback and to inquire about future projects.

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

I hope you're enjoying the final result of your [PROJECT NAME] project. I wanted to follow up and see how everything is going. Your satisfaction is my top priority, and I'm always looking for ways to improve.

Could you spare a moment to provide some feedback on our collaboration? Your insights are invaluable in helping me refine my services.

Additionally, if you have any upcoming projects or know someone who might need design services, I'd be grateful for the referral. You can find more information about my services here: [LINK TO YOUR SERVICES].

Thank you again for the opportunity to work together. I look forward to the possibility of collaborating again in the future.

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 4: The Quick Win Follow-Up

When to use: After a prospect responds with interest but doesn’t commit—reinforce value with a fast, low-effort next step.

Hi [First Name],

Thanks for getting back to me—I’m glad [specific thing they mentioned, e.g., "you're looking to refresh your brand"] caught your attention.

Instead of jumping straight into a sales call, I’d like to offer you something more valuable: a free 15-minute “design audit” of your current [website/branding/marketing materials].

In that time, I’ll:

  • Highlight 2–3 quick improvements that could boost engagement
  • Show you how a cohesive design system can support your goals
  • Answer any questions you have—no pitch, no pressure

If you like what you hear, we can talk next steps. If not, you still walk away with actionable insights.

Does [Day, Time] or [Alternative Day, Time] work for you?

Looking forward to helping you make a stronger impression,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Website URL]

[Your Phone Number, optional]

Template 5: The Portfolio Spotlight Email

When to use: When reaching out to ideal clients—showcase relevant past work without sounding pushy.

Hi [First Name],

I’ve been following [Prospect’s Company Name] for a while and really admire how you [specific compliment, e.g., “blend sustainability with modern aesthetics”].

As a freelance designer who specializes in [your niche, e.g., “clean, conversion-focused brand identities for eco-conscious brands”], I recently helped [Client Name] achieve [specific result, e.g., “a 40% increase in website engagement after a full rebrand”].

Here’s a quick look at that project: [Link to case study/portfolio piece]

I’d love to explore how we could create something equally impactful for [Prospect’s Company Name]. If you're open to a 20-minute chat this week or next, let me know a time that works—or just reply with “Tell me more.”

Either way, keep up the great work,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Portfolio URL]

[LinkedIn or Dribbble Profile, optional]

Template 6: The Post-Meeting Recap & Close

When to use: After a discovery call—reaffirm value and move them toward signing.

Hi [First Name],

It was great chatting earlier about [specific topic discussed, e.g., “revamping your service pages to convert more leads”]. I loved learning about [something unique they shared], and I’m even more excited about the opportunity to help.

To recap what we discussed:

  • Your main goal: [Summarize goal, e.g., “create a more trustworthy, user-friendly experience”]
  • Key pain points: [e.g., “outdated visuals, low time-on-page”]
  • My proposed solution: [e.g., “a mobile-first redesign with stronger visual storytelling”]

Next, I’ll send over a proposal with timelines, deliverables, and investment by [Date + Time].

If you’re ready to move forward, just reply with “Let’s do it,” and I’ll reserve your spot. Projects like this typically book 2–3 weeks out, so I’d love to get started by [Target Start Date].

Thanks again for your time—looking forward to building something great together.

Best,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Email Address]

[Your Phone Number]

Template 7: The “I Noticed…” Cold Outreach

When to use: For warm-ish cold emails where you’ve researched the prospect’s business.

Hi [First Name],

I was browsing [Prospect’s Company Website] and noticed your [specific page/feature, e.g., “product landing page”]—really strong messaging. One thing I did notice: the visual design could better reflect the quality of what you offer.

As a freelance designer who helps [ideal client type, e.g., “SaaS startups convert more free users”], I recently redesigned a similar page for [Client Name], resulting in [specific result, e.g., “a 25% increase in sign-ups”].

Here’s the before & after: [Insert link]

If you’re open to a free, no-pressure 10-minute UX feedback session, I’d be happy to share 2–3 quick design tweaks that could lift conversions.

Interested? Just reply with “Yes, send the feedback.”

Either way, keep up the great work,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Portfolio URL]

Template 8: The Limited Availability Nudge

When to use: When you’re booked but want to stay top-of-mind and create urgency.

Hi [First Name],

Quick update: my client roster is currently full through [Month], but I’ve opened one [early access spot/priority waitlist slot] for designers/brands like yours who value [specific benefit, e.g., “strategic, conversion-driven design”].

If you’re planning to [e.g., “launch a new website or rebrand”] in the next 6–8 weeks, I’d love to reserve time for you before I close bookings completely.

Just reply “Hold my spot,” and I’ll send over next steps—including a free project roadmap to clarify your goals and timeline.

Spots like this fill fast, so let me know by [Date] if you’d like priority access.

Talk soon,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Website URL]

Template 9: The “Here’s What’s Possible” Case Study Pitch

When to use: When a prospect is on the fence—show real results from a similar project.

Hi [First Name],

I know deciding to invest in design can feel risky—especially when you’re not sure what kind of return to expect.

That’s why I want to share what happened with [Client Name], a [similar business type] who was in your exact position 3 months ago.

They were struggling with [specific problem, e.g., “low engagement on their brand materials”], so we redesigned their [specific deliverable, e.g., “brand identity and social templates”] with clarity and consistency in mind.

Results:

  • [Metric 1, e.g., “60% increase in social engagement”]
  • [Metric 2, e.g., “client inquiries up 35%”]
  • [Metric 3, e.g., “launched in just 4 weeks”]

Here’s the full story: [Case Study Link]

If you’re ready to see similar results, I have one opening starting [Start Date]. Let me know if you’d like the proposal sent over—just reply “Send proposal.”

Best,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Portfolio URL]

Template 10: The Referral Thank-You & Incentive

When to use: After a client refers someone—thank them and encourage more.

Hi [First Name],

Just wanted to say a huge thank you for referring [Referred Person’s Name]—I really appreciate you thinking of me!

I’ve reached out to them and will take it from here. As a small thank-you, I’d love to send you [choose one: “a $50 Amazon gift card,” “a free brand audit,” or “a 1-hour design coaching session”]—just let me know your preference.

Also, if you know anyone else who could benefit from [your service, e.g., “a brand that converts”], I’d be honored to help. Every successful referral gets you [repeat the incentive or add: “plus entry into our quarterly $200 bonus draw”].

Grateful for your support,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Website URL]

Template 11: The “I’m Wrapping Up—What’s Next?” Email

When to use: At the end of a project—transition smoothly into retention or upsell.

Hi [First Name],

We’re in the final stretch! I’m wrapping up [Project Name] and will send your final files by [Delivery Date].

Since we’ve built such a strong foundation, I wanted to share two options for what could come next:

  1. Design Retainer: Get ongoing support for [e.g., “monthly graphics, landing pages, or UX tweaks”] with priority turnaround and a 15% discount vs. one-off rates.
  2. Post-Launch Optimization: A 2-week sprint to [e.g., “A/B test key pages, refine CTAs, and boost conversions”] based on real user data.

Both help you keep momentum without the stress of hiring or searching for a new designer.

Want to explore one (or both)? Let’s hop on a quick 15-minute call this week—just reply with your availability.

So proud of what we’ve created together,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Website URL]
