Template 4: Skill Matching Algorithm & Pairing Framework

Instructions: Use this structured approach to match teachers with learners based on skills, availability, and preferences. Adapt the scoring system to prioritize factors most important to your organization.

---

[PROGRAM NAME] Skill Matching Framework

Matching Criteria & Scoring System:

Primary Match Factors (70% of total score):

  • Skill Alignment: [0-25 points]
  • Exact skill match: 25 points
  • Related skill match: 15 points
  • Adjacent skill match: 10 points
  • No match: 0 points
  • Expertise Gap: [0-25 points]
  • Teacher Advanced/Expert + Learner Beginner: 25 points
  • Teacher Intermediate + Learner Beginner: 20 points
  • Teacher Expert + Learner Intermediate: 20 points
  • Teacher Advanced + Learner Intermediate: 15 points
  • Other combinations: 10 points
  • Schedule Compatibility: [0-20 points]
  • Same time zone + overlapping availability: 20 points
  • Different time zone + overlapping availability: 15 points
  • Same time zone + partial overlap: 10 points
  • Different time zone + partial overlap: 5 points
  • No overlap: 0 points

Secondary Match Factors (30% of total score):

  • Department Diversity: [0-10 points]
  • Different departments: 10 points
  • Same department: 5 points
  • Learning Goal Alignment: [0-10 points]
  • High alignment with learner's stated goals: 10 points
  • Medium alignment: 7 points
  • Low alignment: 3 points
  • Format Preference Match: [0-10 points]
  • Exact format preference match: 10 points
  • Compatible format preferences: 7 points
  • Different format preferences: 3 points

Matching Process:

Step 1: Data Preparation

  • Export skills inventory responses to [SPREADSHEET/DATABASE]
  • Clean and standardize skill names
  • Convert time zones to [STANDARD TIMEZONE]

Step 2: Initial Matching

  • Create teacher-learner pairs for each skill area
  • Calculate compatibility scores using the framework above
  • Generate ranked list of potential matches

Step 3: Optimization

  • Ensure each teacher has [MINIMUM] to [MAXIMUM] learners
  • Balance learner distribution across available teachers
  • Consider participant preferences and constraints

Step 4: Manual Review

  • Review matches with scores below [THRESHOLD]
  • Consider special requests or constraints
  • Make final adjustments for optimal program balance

Match Quality Thresholds:

  • Excellent Match: [80-100] points
  • Good Match: [60-79] points
  • Acceptable Match: [40-59] points
  • Requires Review: [Below 40] points
