12 Client Onboarding Email Templates for Freelancers

Updated: February 28, 2026

12 client onboarding email templates for freelancers — ready to use for welcome messages, contract delivery, project kickoff, and information gathering.

What's Inside

  • 12 customizable email templates
  • covers welcome, contract, kickoff, and intake requests
  • designed for quick personalization

Template 1: Welcome and Introduction

When to use: Immediately after a client has agreed to work with you to introduce yourself and set the tone for the project.

Dear [CLIENT NAME],

I am excited to welcome you to [YOUR COMPANY NAME] and thank you for choosing me as your freelancer for this project. My name is [YOUR NAME], and I will be your primary point of contact throughout our collaboration. I am looking forward to working with you to achieve your goals.

Please feel free to reach out to me directly if you have any questions or need any assistance. I am committed to delivering high-quality work and ensuring your complete satisfaction.

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 2: Contract and Agreement Delivery

When to use: After the client has agreed to the terms but before the project starts to formally deliver the contract.

Dear [CLIENT NAME],

I hope this email finds you well. As discussed, I am sending over the contract for our project, which outlines the scope, timeline, and terms of our agreement. Please find the contract attached to this email.

If you have any questions or need clarification on any of the points, do not hesitate to reach out. I am more than happy to address them. Once you are ready, please sign and return one copy to me by [DEADLINE FOR RETURN].

Thank you for your prompt attention to this matter.

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 3: Project Kickoff and Next Steps

When to use: To officially start the project and inform the client about the next steps.

Dear [CLIENT NAME],

I am pleased to inform you that we are moving forward with your project as planned. The next steps will involve [BRIEFLY MENTION THE IMMEDIATE NEXT STEPS, SUCH AS INFORMATION GATHERING OR INITIAL RESEARCH].

To ensure a smooth process, I would appreciate it if you could provide me with [SPECIFIC INFORMATION OR MATERIALS NEEDED FROM THE CLIENT]. This will enable me to better understand your requirements and deliver work that meets your expectations.

If you have any questions or concerns, please do not hesitate to contact me. I am always here to help.

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 4: Information Gathering for Project Success

When to use: When you need specific information from the client to proceed with the project effectively.

Dear [CLIENT NAME],

As we progress with your project, I require some additional information to ensure that the final product meets your needs and expectations. Could you please provide me with the following details: [LIST SPECIFIC INFORMATION NEEDED, SUCH AS LOGOS, COLOR SCHEMES, OR SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS].

Your prompt response will help me stay on schedule and deliver high-quality work. If you have any questions about the information requested, please feel free to ask.

Thank you for your cooperation and understanding.

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 5: Setting Up Communication Channels

When to use: To establish how you will communicate with the client throughout the project.

Dear [CLIENT NAME],

To ensure effective communication throughout our project, I wanted to touch base with you regarding our communication channels. My preferred method of communication is [STATE PREFERRED METHOD, SUCH AS EMAIL OR PROJECT MANAGEMENT TOOL], but I am flexible and can adapt to your preferences.

For urgent matters, please do not hesitate to contact me directly at [YOUR PHONE NUMBER]. For all other inquiries or updates, [PROVIDE DETAILS ON HOW YOU WILL SHARE INFORMATION AND HOW OFTEN].

Let me know if this arrangement works for you, or if you have any suggestions for how we can best communicate.

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

[YOUR NAME]

Template 8: Request for Additional Information Due to Scope Change

When to use: When the client requests changes that affect the project's scope and you need more information to accommodate these changes.

Your prompt response will help me to provide you with an updated project plan and any necessary adjustments to the scope, timeline, or costs.
