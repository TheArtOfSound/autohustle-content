Template 4: Financial Advisor Thought Leadership Article

When to use: For long-form articles positioning a financial professional as an expert in retirement, investing, or wealth planning

[HEADLINE: e.g., 5 Wealth-Building Mistakes Retirees Make (And How to Avoid Them)]

By [ADVISOR NAME], [CREDENTIALS] at [FIRM NAME]

As you approach or enter retirement, managing your finances becomes less about growth and more about preservation—and strategy. Yet many well-prepared individuals still fall into common traps that erode their savings over time. Here are five mistakes I see in my practice at [FIRM NAME], and how you can steer clear of them.

[MISTAKE 1: e.g., Withdrawing Too Much Too Soon]

Many retirees assume a 4% annual withdrawal is “safe”—but market volatility can make this unsustainable. Instead, consider a [ALTERNATIVE STRATEGY, e.g., dynamic withdrawal model] that adjusts based on portfolio performance.

[MISTAKE 2: e.g., Ignoring Tax Diversification]

Holding only traditional IRAs or 401(k)s can lead to tax bombs in retirement. A balanced mix of taxable, tax-deferred, and Roth accounts gives you control over your tax bracket each year.

[MISTAKE 3: e.g., Underestimating Healthcare Costs]

The average couple retiring at 65 will spend [DOLLAR AMOUNT] on healthcare—not including long-term care. Planning ahead with an HSA or long-term care insurance can prevent financial strain.

[MISTAKE 4: e.g., Failing to Update Beneficiaries]

Life changes—marriages, divorces, births—but beneficiary designations often don’t. Review these annually with your advisor to ensure your assets go where you intend.

[MISTAKE 5: e.g., Letting Emotions Drive Decisions]

Market downturns can trigger panic selling. A disciplined, rules-based investment strategy helps you stay the course.

[CALL TO ACTION]

Want a personalized retirement income plan? [Schedule a complimentary 30-minute consultation with [ADVISOR NAME]] or [download our free checklist: “7 Must-Do Financial Moves Before 65”].
