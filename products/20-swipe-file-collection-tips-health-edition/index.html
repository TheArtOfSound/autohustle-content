1. Healthy Meal Planning Template

Use case: When creating personalized nutrition plans for clients

"Start by assessing your client's dietary needs and preferences. Create a template that includes space for breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snack options. Consider including a section for nutritional information, such as macronutrient breakdowns and calorie counts. Example:

Monday:

  • Breakfast: Overnight oats with fruit and nuts (300 calories, 30g protein)
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken salad with quinoa and vegetables (400 calories, 50g protein)
  • Dinner: Baked salmon with sweet potato and green beans (500 calories, 60g protein)"

2. Workout Routine for Beginners

Use case: When designing fitness plans for new clients

"Create a routine that includes 3-4 days of exercise per week, with at least one day of rest in between. Start with short workouts (20-30 minutes) and gradually increase duration and intensity. Example:

Monday (Upper Body):

  • Warm-up: 5-minute walk or jog
  • Push-ups: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Bicep curls: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Cool-down: 5-minute stretching"

3. Mindful Meditation Script

Use case: When leading mindfulness sessions or creating guided meditations

"Begin by finding a comfortable seated or lying position. Close your eyes and take a deep breath in through your nose, and out through your mouth. Focus on the sensation of the breath, feeling the air move in and out of the body. Example:

'Imagine yourself standing on a peaceful beach at sunset. Notice the sound of the waves, the feeling of the sand beneath your feet, and the warmth of the sun on your skin. As you breathe in, imagine fresh, calm air filling your lungs. As you breathe out, imagine any tension or stress leaving your body.'"

4. Nutrition Myth-Busting Handout

Use case: When educating clients about common nutrition misconceptions

"Create a handout that addresses common myths and misconceptions about nutrition. Example:

Myth: Low-fat foods are always healthy

Reality: Many low-fat foods are highly processed and may contain added sugars or artificial ingredients

Myth: Eating after 8pm will lead to weight gain

Reality: The timing of meals does not directly impact weight gain, but rather overall calorie intake and nutrient balance"

5. High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) Workout

Use case: When creating high-energy fitness routines for advanced clients

"Design a workout that includes short bursts of high-intensity exercise followed by brief periods of rest. Example:

Warm-up: 5-minute jog or jump rope

Sprints: 30 seconds of all-out effort, followed by 30 seconds of rest

Burpees: 30 seconds of all-out effort, followed by 30 seconds of rest

Cool-down: 5-minute stretching"

6. Sleep Hygiene Tips

Use case: When advising clients on improving sleep quality

"Create a list of tips for improving sleep hygiene, such as:

  • Establish a consistent sleep schedule
  • Create a dark, quiet sleep environment
  • Avoid screens and electronics before bedtime
  • Avoid stimulating activities before bedtime
  • Try relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing or meditation, before sleep"

7. Progressive Muscle Relaxation Technique

Use case: When teaching clients relaxation techniques for stress management

"Have the client find a comfortable position and take a deep breath in. Then, tense and relax different muscle groups in the body, starting with the toes and moving up to the head. Example:

'Tense the muscles in your toes for 5 seconds, and then release. Feel the relaxation spread through your feet and up your legs. Next, tense the muscles in your calves for 5 seconds, and then release. Continue this process, moving up through the body, tensing and relaxing each muscle group in turn.'"

17. Healthy Grocery Shopping Tips

Use case: When advising clients on making healthy food choices

1. Morning Routine Optimization Script

Use case: When coaching patients on establishing a consistent, health-supportive morning routine

Start your day with purpose: within 30 minutes of waking, drink 16 oz of water with a pinch of electrolytes, perform 5 minutes of deep breathing or light stretching, and eat a protein-rich breakfast. Avoid screens for the first 60 minutes. This routine stabilizes cortisol, enhances insulin sensitivity, and sets a proactive tone for the day.

2. Hydration Reminder Template

Use case: For patient follow-ups or wellness plan handouts

Did you know even mild dehydration (as little as 1–2%) can impair cognitive function and mood? Aim to drink half your body weight (in pounds) in ounces of water daily. Set hourly phone alerts or use a marked water bottle to stay on track. Add lemon, cucumber, or mint for flavor—no sugary drinks needed.

3. Medication Adherence Follow-Up Message

Use case: After prescribing a new medication

It’s common to forget or hesitate with a new medication. If you’ve missed a dose or experienced side effects, please don’t stop taking it—call us first. We can adjust timing, dosage, or explore alternatives. Consistency saves lives: 50% of patients stop taking prescribed meds within one year. You’re not alone—we’re here to help.

4. Blood Pressure Log Instruction

Use case: When advising patients to monitor BP at home

Take your blood pressure twice daily—once in the morning (after bathroom, before breakfast) and once at night (same time each day). Sit quietly for 5 minutes, feet flat, arm supported at heart level. Record each reading in your log or app. Bring this log to your next visit—we’ll use it to fine-tune your care plan.

5. Sleep Hygiene Handout Snippet

Use case: For patients with insomnia or poor sleep quality

Your brain needs a wind-down signal. One hour before bed: dim lights, stop screen use, and engage in a low-stimulus activity (reading, gentle stretching, journaling). Keep your bedroom cool (65–68°F), dark, and quiet. No exceptions—even on weekends. Consistent sleep hygiene improves sleep onset and deep sleep by up to 30%.

6. Post-Visit Action Plan Summary

Use case: After a consultation to reinforce next steps

Here’s your personalized action plan:

  1. Start daily 30-minute walks, 5 days/week
  2. Replace sugary snacks with whole fruit and nuts
  3. Monitor fasting glucose every Monday morning
  4. Schedule follow-up in 4 weeks

Write this on your fridge. Small steps create lasting change—we believe in your progress.

7. Chronic Pain Management Reminder

Use case: For patients managing long-term musculoskeletal pain

Pain flares are not failures. Pause, assess your activity level, hydration, and sleep quality. Gentle movement (like walking or tai chi) often helps more than complete rest. Use heat/cold therapy as directed. If pain persists beyond 72 hours or worsens, contact us—don’t tough it out.

8. Lab Result Explanation Script

Use case: When explaining borderline or abnormal results

Your HbA1c is slightly elevated (5.9%), which indicates increased risk for type 2 diabetes—but not a diagnosis. This is a warning sign we can act on now. With modest weight loss (5–7% of body weight) and 150 minutes of weekly activity, you can reduce your risk by over 50%. Let’s build your prevention plan today.

9. Vaccination Talking Point

Use case: Addressing vaccine hesitancy in adult patients

Vaccines aren’t just for kids. Adults need Tdap, flu, shingles, and pneumococcal vaccines too. These prevent serious complications that hospitalize 170,000+ adults annually. The risks of the diseases far outweigh the minimal side effects of vaccination. Let’s review your immunization history and update what’s needed.

10. Nutrition Label Reading Guide

Use case: Teaching patients to make informed food choices

When reading labels, focus on three things:

  1. Serving size (is it realistic?)
  2. Added sugars (aim for <25g/day)
  3. Ingredients list (shorter is better; avoid unpronounceable items)

If the first three ingredients include sugar, corn syrup, or hydrogenated oil—put it back. Your body deserves real food.

11. Stress Reduction Mini-Protocol

Use case: For patients reporting high stress or burnout

Try the 4-7-8 breathing method: inhale quietly through your nose for 4 seconds, hold for 7 seconds, exhale completely through your mouth for 8 seconds. Repeat 4 times, 2x daily. This activates your parasympathetic nervous system, lowering heart rate and cortisol. Use it before meals or bedtime.

12. Physical Activity Prescription

Use case: When encouraging sedentary patients to start moving

You don’t need a gym. Start with 10-minute walks after meals—three times a day. That’s 30 minutes, which meets national guidelines. Add resistance (cans, resistance bands) twice weekly. Every step counts: walking 150 minutes/week reduces cardiovascular risk by 30%.

13. Patient Empowerment Statement

Use case: To reinforce self-efficacy during consultations

You are the expert on your body. Our role is to guide, support, and provide evidence-based options. You make the decisions. When you take informed action—even small changes—you improve outcomes more than any prescription alone. Your voice matters in this process.

14. Fall Prevention Talking Points

Use case: For elderly patients or those with mobility issues

Falls are not a normal part of aging. Reduce your risk: install grab bars in the bathroom, improve home lighting, wear non-slip shoes, and review medications that may cause dizziness. Balance exercises like standing on one foot (30 seconds, twice daily) strengthen stability. Let’s assess your risk today.

15. Gut Health Conversation Starter

Use case: When discussing digestive issues or microbiome support

70% of your immune system lives in your gut. Support it: eat 25–30g of fiber daily from diverse plant sources (beans, berries, broccoli), limit processed foods, and consider a probiotic if you’ve taken antibiotics recently. Fermented foods like kefir, sauerkraut, and kimchi are natural allies.

16. Smoking Cessation Support Message

Use case: During routine visits with smokers

Quitting smoking is the single most impactful change you can make for your health. After 1 year, your cardiovascular risk drops by half. We offer counseling, nicotine replacement, and prescription options—no judgment, just support. Let’s create your quit plan together, at your pace.

17. Weight Management Reality Check

Use case: For patients frustrated with slow progress

Sustainable weight loss is 1–2 pounds per week. Rapid loss often leads to rebound. Focus on habits: consistent protein intake, sleep, and activity. A 5–10% reduction in body weight significantly improves blood pressure, glucose, and joint pain. Progress, not perfection, is the goal.

18. Mental Health Screening Follow-Up

Use case: After a positive PHQ-9 or GAD-7 score

Your answers suggest you may be experiencing depression/anxiety—this is more common than you think, and highly treatable. It’s not weakness, and you’re not alone. Therapy, lifestyle changes, and sometimes medication can make a profound difference. Let’s discuss your options with compassion and confidentiality.

19. Post-Discharge Care Reminder

Use case: After hospital discharge

The first 72 hours at home are critical. Take all medications as prescribed, watch for warning signs (fever, shortness of breath, swelling), and attend your follow-up within 7 days. Keep a list of your meds and symptoms. If something feels off—call us. We’re your safety net.
