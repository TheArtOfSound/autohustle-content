Troubleshooting Common Issues

Problem: Generic or Bland Outputs

Solution:

  • Add more specific context about your business
  • Include emotional triggers relevant to your audience
  • Specify the tone and style you want
  • Reference current events or trends in your industry

Example Fix:

```

Instead of: "Write about productivity tips"

Try: "Write about productivity tips for overwhelmed freelance graphic designers who struggle with client communication while managing multiple projects"

```

Problem: Outputs Don't Match Your Brand Voice

Solution:

  • Create a brand voice primer to include with prompts
  • Provide examples of your existing content
  • Specify what to avoid in tone and language
  • Use voice modifiers in your prompts

Brand Voice Primer Template:

```

My brand voice is: [adjectives]

I always: [behaviors/approaches]

I never: [things to avoid]

My audience responds well to: [communication styles]

```

Problem: Repetitive or Similar Content

Solution:

  • Rotate between different prompt categories
  • Vary your input parameters
  • Combine prompts from different sections
  • Use time-based or seasonal modifiers

Problem: Content Feels Too Sales-Heavy

Solution:

  • Focus on value-first prompts
  • Use educational and entertainment categories
  • Add storytelling elements
  • Include community-building prompts

Format and Style Guide

Prompt Structure

Each prompt follows this optimized format:

Context Setting: Establishes the scenario and audience

Specific Task: Clear, actionable instruction

Output Parameters: Desired length, format, and style

Success Criteria: What makes the output effective

Style Guidelines

For Social Media Content:

  • Keep initial outputs under 280 characters for Twitter adaptation
  • Include hashtag suggestions
  • Add engagement hooks (questions, calls-to-action)
  • Consider visual content opportunities

For Long-Form Content:

  • Start with compelling headlines
  • Include subheadings and bullet points
  • Add personal anecdotes or case studies
  • End with clear next steps

For Email Content:

  • Write compelling subject lines
  • Use conversational, personal tone
  • Include clear value propositions
  • Add specific calls-to-action

Quality Checkpoints

Before publishing any AI-generated content:

✅ Accuracy Check: Verify all facts and claims

✅ Brand Alignment: Matches your voice and values

✅ Value Assessment: Provides genuine value to your audience

✅ Originality: Adds your unique perspective or experience

✅ Engagement Factor: Likely to generate interaction or response

Ready-to-Use Prompt Collection

Category 1: Brand Storytelling & Personal Narrative

#### Prompt 1: Origin Story Catalyst

```

Act as a brand storytelling expert. Help me craft a compelling origin story for my [business/service] that resonates with [target audience]. Focus on the specific moment or challenge that sparked my entrepreneurial journey. Include the emotional transformation, the "aha moment," and how it directly connects to the value I provide today. Write in a conversational, authentic tone that could work for an About page, social media post, or speaking introduction. Keep it under 200 words but pack it with emotional resonance and relatability.

```

#### Prompt 2: Behind-the-Scenes Vulnerability

```

Create a behind-the-scenes post that shows the real, unpolished side of running my [type of business]. Focus on a recent challenge, mistake, or learning moment that my audience of [target audience] would find relatable and valuable. Include the lesson learned and how it's made me better at serving my clients/customers. Write it as if I'm having a genuine conversation with a friend who's also an entrepreneur. Include a thought-provoking question at the end to encourage engagement.

```

#### Prompt 3: Values in Action

```

Help me write about how one of my core business values [specify value] played out in a real client situation or business decision. Show don't tell - use specific details and dialogue where appropriate. Make it clear how this value translates into tangible benefits for my [target audience]. End with how readers can apply this same principle in their own [work/business/life]. Write in a storytelling format that could work as a LinkedIn post or newsletter story.

```

#### Prompt 4: Transformation Showcase

```

Create a post highlighting a specific transformation or result I've helped a client achieve. Focus on the before/after but emphasize the process and mindset shifts that made the change possible. Include enough detail that other [target audience members] can see themselves in this story. Protect client privacy by changing identifying details while keeping the core transformation authentic. End with an insight about what makes transformations like this possible.

```

Category 2: Educational Content & Thought Leadership

#### Prompt 5: Myth-Busting Authority

```

Identify a common misconception in [your industry] that holds back [your target audience] and write a myth-busting post that establishes my expertise. Start with "Most people believe [myth], but here's what actually works..." Include 3-4 specific points that debunk the myth with practical alternatives. Use examples or mini case studies to illustrate each point. Write with confidence but not arrogance, positioning me as a helpful guide rather than a know-it-all.

```

#### Prompt 6: Framework Creator

```

Help me create and explain a simple, memorable framework for [specific challenge your audience faces]. Give the framework a catchy name and break it into 3-5 clear steps or components. For each component, provide a brief explanation and a practical example of how to implement it. Write it so that readers can immediately start using this framework. Include a note about how this framework has evolved from my experience working with [type of clients/customers].

```

#### Prompt 7: Trend Analysis & Future-Casting

```

Write an analysis of an emerging trend in [your industry] and its implications for [your target audience]. Explain what the trend is, why it matters, and provide 3 specific, actionable ways my audience can prepare for or capitalize on this trend. Include my unique perspective based on my experience in [your specific area of expertise]. End with a prediction about where this trend is heading and what it means for the future of [relevant field/industry].

```

#### Prompt 8: Problem-Solution Deep Dive

```

Take a complex problem that [your target audience] faces and break it down into understandable components. Explain why this problem exists, why traditional solutions often fail, and present a fresh approach or solution. Include specific steps or strategies that readers can implement immediately. Use analogies or metaphors to make complex concepts accessible. Position this as insights gained from working with multiple clients facing similar challenges.

```

Category 3: Community Building & Engagement

#### Prompt 9: Conversation Starter

```

Create a thought-provoking post that starts a meaningful conversation within my community of [target audience]. Begin with an observation about [relevant industry/topic], then pose 2-3 questions that don't have obvious answers. Make it clear that I'm genuinely interested in different perspectives and experiences. Include my own initial thoughts to model the kind of sharing I'm hoping to see. End with an invitation for people to share their experiences in the comments.

```

#### Prompt 10: Community Celebration

```

Write a post celebrating and highlighting members of my community who are doing amazing work in [relevant field]. Feature 2-3 specific people (you can use placeholder names), describing what makes their approach or achievements noteworthy. Connect their success back to principles or values that resonate with the broader community. End with an invitation for others to share their wins or nominate other community members who deserve recognition.

```

#### Prompt 11: Shared Experience Connector

```

Identify a universal experience that most [target audience members] have had in their [work/business/creative journey] and write about it in a way that makes people feel seen and understood. Start with "Can we talk about..." and describe the experience with specific, relatable details. Acknowledge both the challenges and the growth that comes from this experience. End with an invitation for others to share how they've navigated similar situations.

```

#### Prompt 12: Resource Roundup & Recommendations

```

Create a curated list of resources (tools, books, podcasts, etc.) that I genuinely recommend for [specific challenge or goal relevant to your audience]. For each recommendation, explain why I chose it and how it specifically helps with [the challenge/goal]. Include a mix of well-known and lesser-known resources. Add my personal experience with each resource where relevant. Frame it as "Resources that have genuinely made a difference in my [work/business] and might help you too."

```

Category 4: Business Development & Client Attraction

#### Prompt 13: Social Proof Storyteller

```

Transform a client testimonial or success story into engaging content that attracts similar clients. Focus on the client's initial situation, the specific way I helped them, and the measurable results they achieved. Include enough detail that prospects can envision working with me, but keep the focus on the client's transformation rather than my services. End with a subtle indication of how readers facing similar challenges might get similar results.

```

#### Prompt 14: Process Transparency

```

Explain my unique approach or methodology for [your main service/product] in a way that builds trust and demonstrates expertise without giving away everything. Break down my process into clear phases or steps, explaining the reasoning behind each phase and what clients can expect. Include what makes my approach different from others in the field. Write it as educational content that positions me as the obvious choice when readers are ready to get help.

```

#### Prompt 15: Objection Handler

```

Address a common objection or concern that prevents [target audience] from [taking desired action - hiring you, buying your product, etc.]. Start by acknowledging the concern as valid, then provide a thoughtful response that includes examples or evidence. Share a story about a client who had the same concern and how it worked out. End with a reframe that helps readers see the situation differently. Write with empathy and understanding, not defensiveness.

```

#### Prompt 16: Value Demonstration

```

Create content that demonstrates the value of [your service/expertise] by sharing a specific insight, tip, or mini-training that gives readers a genuine win. Make it substantial enough that people get real value, but position it as just one piece of the larger puzzle you help clients solve. Include a brief mention of how this fits into the bigger picture of what you do. End with an invitation for readers who want to go deeper to reach out.

```

Category 5: Industry Insights & Commentary

#### Prompt 17: Industry Evolution Observer

```

Write about how [your industry] has changed over the past [timeframe] and what it means for [your target audience]. Include specific examples of changes you've witnessed and how they've affected the way you work with clients or approach your business. Offer predictions about where things are heading and how your audience can stay ahead of the curve. Position yourself as someone who's been paying attention and can help others navigate these changes.

```

#### Prompt 18: Contrarian Viewpoint

```

Present a respectful but contrarian viewpoint on a popular opinion or trend in [your industry]. Explain why you disagree with the conventional wisdom, providing specific reasons and examples. Acknowledge the merits of the popular viewpoint while making your case for a different approach. Include how this contrarian perspective has benefited your work or clients. Write with conviction but remain open to dialogue and different perspectives.

```

#### Prompt 19: Cross-Industry Insight

```

Draw connections between [your industry] and another field or industry, highlighting lessons that [your target audience] can apply to their work. Explain what [other industry] does differently and how those principles could revolutionize the way we approach [relevant challenge in your industry]. Include specific examples of how you've applied these cross-industry insights in your own work. Make it practical and actionable for your audience.

```

#### Prompt 20: Future Skills Predictor

```

Identify skills that will become increasingly important for [your target audience] in the next 2-3 years and explain why. For each skill, provide specific examples of how it's already becoming relevant and practical steps people can take to start developing it now. Include how you're personally preparing for these changes and what you're seeing among the most successful people in your field. End with encouragement about the opportunities these changes will create.

```

Category 6: Productivity & Mindset

#### Prompt 21: Productivity System Breakdown

```

Share a specific productivity system, tool, or habit that has significantly improved your effectiveness as a [your role/business type]. Explain exactly how you implement it, including any modifications you've made to fit your specific needs. Include before/after results and be honest about what didn't work initially. Provide enough detail that readers could implement the same system, but acknowledge that they may need to adapt it for their situation.

```

#### Prompt 22: Mindset Shift Catalyst

```

Describe a specific mindset shift that transformed how you approach [relevant aspect of business/work] and led to better results. Explain what you used to believe, what triggered the shift, and how you think about it now. Include concrete examples of how this new mindset plays out in daily decisions or client work. Help readers identify if they might be operating from the old mindset and provide guidance on making the shift themselves.

```

#### Prompt 23: Boundary Setting Guide

```

Share how you've learned to set and maintain healthy boundaries in your [work/business], including specific examples of boundaries you've implemented and how you communicate them. Address common concerns like fear of losing clients or appearing unprofessional. Include the positive results you've seen from better boundaries, both for yourself and your clients. Provide practical language readers can use to set similar boundaries in their own situations.

```

#### Prompt 24: Decision-Making Framework

```

Explain your personal framework for making difficult business decisions, using a recent example to illustrate how it works. Break down your decision-making process into steps that others could follow, including how you gather information, weigh options, and handle uncertainty. Include how you deal with decision paralysis or conflicting advice. Make it practical enough that readers could apply this framework to their own challenging decisions.

```
