Template 8: Brand Voice & Messaging Guide (Internal Template)

When to use: To align team members and freelancers on brand tone, values, and key messaging

Brand Name: [BRAND NAME]

Tagline (if applicable): [TAGLINE]

Our Mission

[BRAND NAME] exists to [MISSION STATEMENT — E.G., empower small businesses with simple, beautiful tools for growth].

Target Audience

We serve [DEMOGRAPHIC, E.G., female entrepreneurs ages 30–45] who [NEED/DESIRE, E.G., want to scale their side hustle without burnout].

Brand Personality

Choose 3-5 traits:

☑️ Friendly

☑️ Professional

☑️ Playful

☑️ Trustworthy

☑️ Innovative

Tone of Voice

  • In customer service: [E.G., Empathetic, prompt, solution-focused]
  • On social media: [E.G., Lighthearted, encouraging, conversational]
  • In marketing emails: [E.G., Clear, benefit-driven, warm]

Key Messaging Pillars

  1. [PILLAR 1, E.G., Simplicity]: We believe [BRIEF EXPLANATION]
  2. [PILLAR 2, E.G., Quality]: Every [PRODUCT] is designed to [BENEFIT]
  3. [PILLAR 3, E.G., Community]: We support [TARGET AUDIENCE] by [ACTION]

Do’s and Don’ts

| Do | Don’t |

|----|-------|

| Use contractions (we’re, you’ll) | Use jargon or complex terms |

| Focus on customer benefits | Talk only about features |

| Be warm and inclusive | Sound robotic or salesy |

Example Phrases

✅ “We’ve got your back.”

✅ “Simple solutions for busy [TARGET AUDIENCE].”

🚫 “Our cutting-edge, enterprise-grade solution…”

🚫 “Contact us for a synergistic paradigm shift.”

Use this guide for all customer-facing content — from emails to ads to social posts.

Template 5: Email Newsletter Introduction

When to use: For the introductory section of a monthly or quarterly newsletter to engage subscribers.

"Dear [SUBSCRIBER NAME], welcome to our [MONTHLY/QUARTERLY] newsletter! We're excited to share the latest updates from [COMPANY NAME], including new [PRODUCTS/SERVICES], upcoming [EVENTS], and valuable [INDUSTRY INSIGHTS]. In this issue, you'll find [BRIEF SUMMARY OF NEWSLETTER CONTENT]. We hope you enjoy it and look forward to your feedback!"

Template 6: Customer Testimonial Request

When to use: To request feedback from satisfied customers for marketing purposes.

"Dear [CUSTOMER NAME], we hope you're enjoying [PRODUCT/SERVICE NAME]! We're always looking for ways to improve and would love to hear about your experience. Could you spare a minute to share your thoughts on [SPECIFIC ASPECT OF PRODUCT/SERVICE]? Your feedback will help us better serve you and others. If you're willing, we'd also love to feature your testimonial on our [WEBSITE/SOCIAL MEDIA] to inspire others. Please reply to this email or contact us at [CONTACT EMAIL]."

Template 7: Event Invitation

When to use: To invite customers, partners, or the public to an event, webinar, or workshop.

"You're Invited! Join us for [EVENT NAME] on [DATE] at [TIME] at [LOCATION/VIRTUAL EVENT LINK]. This [EVENT TYPE] is designed to [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF EVENT AND ITS BENEFITS]. Our keynote speaker, [SPEAKER NAME], will discuss [TOPIC]. Register now and take advantage of this opportunity to [CALL TO ACTION]. Limited spots available! RSVP by [RSVP DEADLINE] to secure your place. Contact us at [CONTACT EMAIL] for more information."

Template 8: Press Release Introduction

When to use: For the opening section of a press release to grab the attention of media outlets and the public.

"[CITY, STATE] – [COMPANY NAME], a [COMPANY DESCRIPTION], today announced [ANNOUNCEMENT TYPE], a [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF ANNOUNCEMENT]. This [ANNOUNCEMENT TYPE] marks a significant [IMPACT/ACHIEVEMENT] for [INDUSTRY/COMMUNITY], enabling [KEY BENEFIT]. For more information, please contact [CONTACT NAME] at [CONTACT EMAIL] or [CONTACT PHONE]."

Template 9: Blog Post Outline

When to use: To structure and organize content for a blog post on a specific topic.

"I. Introduction to [TOPIC]

  • Brief overview of [TOPIC]
  • Importance of [TOPIC] in [INDUSTRY]

II. [SUBTOPIC 1]

  • Detailed explanation of [SUBTOPIC 1]
  • Examples or case studies related to [SUBTOPIC 1]

III. [SUBTOPIC 2]

  • In-depth analysis of [SUBTOPIC 2]
  • Tips or best practices for [SUBTOPIC 2]

IV. Conclusion

  • Summary of key points
  • Call to action for readers to [SPECIFIC ACTION]"

When to use: For the introductory section of a sales page to engage potential customers.

"Are you tired of [COMMON PROBLEM]? Do you wish there was a way to [DESIRED OUTCOME]? Introducing [PRODUCT/SERVICE NAME], the [PRODUCT/SERVICE CATEGORY] designed to [KEY BENEFIT]. With [PRODUCT/SERVICE NAME], you can [SPECIFIC CAPABILITY] and achieve [DESIRED RESULT]. Say goodbye to [UNDESIRED SITUATION] and hello to [DESIRED SITUATION]. Keep reading to learn how [PRODUCT/SERVICE NAME] can help you [ACHIEVE GOAL]."
