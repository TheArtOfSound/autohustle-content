Template 3: Round 2 Revision Request with Specific Questions

When to use: After receiving vague initial feedback (e.g., “I don’t like it”) and needing clearer direction

Hi [Client Name],

Thanks for your feedback on the [Project Name] draft. I appreciate you sharing your thoughts.

To help me make the right adjustments, could you clarify a few things?

  1. When you said “[Quote vague feedback, e.g., ‘It feels off’],” were you reacting to the [color palette / layout / typography / imagery]?
  2. Is there a specific brand or design you’ve seen that better matches your vision?
  3. What emotion or message should this design communicate? (e.g., “trustworthy,” “playful,” “luxurious”)

The more specific you can be, the better I can refine the next version to meet your expectations.

I’d love to have your responses by [Date] so I can deliver the revised draft by [Revision Delivery Date].

Thanks again!

Template 4: Feedback Submission Guidelines (Client Onboarding)

When to use: Sent to new clients at project start to set clear expectations for how to give feedback

Hi [Client Name],

Welcome to the [Project Name] project! I’m excited to collaborate with you.

To make our process as smooth as possible, I’ve outlined how to share feedback effectively:

✅ Do:

  • Be specific: Instead of “I don’t like this,” try “The font feels too playful for our professional brand.”
  • Reference brand guidelines: Remind me of your core values or audience when possible.
  • Use comments in [Figma/Adobe XD/Shared Platform] for pinpoint feedback.
  • Group feedback into one message to avoid mixed signals.

❌ Avoid:

  • Changing core goals mid-project without discussing scope impact
  • Involving too many stakeholders without a final decision-maker
  • Waiting more than [X] days to respond

Let me know if you have any questions! I’m here to make this process easy and productive for you.

Best,
