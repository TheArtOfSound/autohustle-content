1. Daily Priority Matrix

Use case: When planning your workday to focus on high-impact tasks

Each morning, write down your top 6 tasks. Categorize them using the Eisenhower Matrix:

  • Urgent & Important: Do first
  • Important but Not Urgent: Schedule
  • Urgent but Not Important: Delegate
  • Neither: Eliminate

Example:

  1. Prepare client presentation (Urgent & Important) → Do at 9 AM
  2. Weekly team sync (Important, Not Urgent) → Schedule for 2 PM
  3. Answer non-critical emails (Urgent, Not Important) → Delegate to assistant
  4. Update software dashboard (Neither) → Eliminate or batch weekly

2. The 90-Minute Focus Block

Use case: When tackling deep work without distractions

Schedule one 90-minute uninterrupted block each day for high-concentration tasks. Turn off notifications, close email, and use a physical timer.

Example text for calendar invite:

Focus Block: Deep Work – Q3 Strategy Draft

Time: 8:00–9:30 AM

Location: No meetings. Phone on DND. Slack status: “In Focus Mode”

Outcome: Complete first draft of strategy document

3. Two-Minute Rule Implementation Script

Use case: When reviewing your task list and avoiding procrastination

Apply David Allen’s two-minute rule: if a task takes less than two minutes, do it immediately.

Example checklist:

  • Reply to approval request ✅ Done
  • Forward contract to legal ✅ Done
  • Schedule follow-up call ✅ Done
  • Draft project brief (30 min) → Add to tomorrow’s plan

Use this script at the end of each meeting or email review session.

4. Weekly Review Template

Use case: Every Friday to assess progress and plan the next week

Conduct a 30-minute weekly review using this structure:

  1. Review completed tasks (check off in tracker)
  2. Identify incomplete items – reschedule or delete
  3. Review upcoming deadlines and meetings
  4. Set 3 top priorities for next week
  5. Clear inbox and digital desktop

Example:

✅ Completed: Client onboarding, team 1:1s, expense report

🔄 Moved: Finalize proposal → Monday 10 AM

🎯 Next Week Priorities: 1) Deliver Q3 results deck, 2) Hire contractor, 3) Audit tool stack

5. SMART Goal Framework

Use case: When setting quarterly or project-based objectives

Use the SMART acronym to refine vague goals into actionable targets.

Example:

❌ “Improve team productivity”

✅ “Increase team output by 15% over Q4 by implementing automated reporting (Oct 1–Dec 31), measured via weekly task completion metrics in Asana.”

Break down:

  • Specific: Increase team output
  • Measurable: 15% improvement
  • Achievable: With automation tools
  • Relevant: Aligns with ops efficiency goal
  • Time-bound: Q4 deadline

6. Task Batching Schedule

Use case: When overwhelmed by constant task-switching

Group similar low-cognition tasks into time blocks to reduce mental load.

Example daily batch schedule:

  • 10:00–10:20: Email triage (only twice daily)
  • 12:30–1:00: Admin work (invoices, approvals)
  • 4:00–4:15: Slack catch-up
  • 5:00–5:20: Calendar management

Use recurring calendar events to enforce discipline.

Agenda:

  1. Review Q3 results (10 min)

  3. Assign owners & deadlines (10 min)

Prep required: Bring 1 initiative idea and status update

9. Productivity Audit Questionnaire

Use case: When evaluating your current workflow efficiency

Run a self-audit weekly with these 5 questions:

  1. What task took longer than expected? Why?
  2. When did I feel most focused? What enabled it?
  3. What interrupted my flow? (e.g., notifications, meetings)
  4. Which tool or process felt clunky?
  5. What one change would save me 1 hour this week?

Example response:

  1. Weekly report – manual data pulls
  2. 8–9:30 AM – no meetings, phone off
  3. Slack pings during deep work
  4. Exporting from CRM
  5. Automate data export with Zapier

| Reduce meeting load | 30% ↓ | -18% | 60% | Nov 30 | Alex |

11. Pomodoro Technique Script

Use case: When starting a task you’ve been avoiding

Use the Pomodoro Technique: 25 minutes focused work, 5-minute break. After 4 cycles, take a 30-minute break.

Example timer script:

“Start Pomodoro 1: Draft onboarding guide” → Set timer

After 25 min: “Review output. Take 5-min break – stretch, no screens.”

After 4 Pomodoros: “Long break – walk outside for 30 min.”

12. Decision-Making Flowchart

Use case: When facing recurring low-stakes decisions

Use this flow for routine choices:

Need to decide? → Is it reversible?

→ Yes → Make it now (under 2 min)

→ No → Is it high-impact?

→ Yes → Gather data, consult, decide in 24h

→ No → Flip a coin (or pick randomly)

Example: Choosing meeting time slots – reversible, low-impact → pick one now.

13. Energy Mapping Exercise

Use case: When optimizing your work schedule to match natural rhythms

Track your energy hourly for 3 days using a 1–5 scale. Then align tasks:

  • 5 = peak focus → deep work
  • 3–4 = medium → meetings, collaboration
  • 1–2 = low → admin, email

Example pattern:

8–10 AM: Energy 5 → Writing, strategy

10–12 PM: Energy 4 → Team syncs

1–2 PM: Energy 3 → Client calls

3–5 PM: Energy 2 → Email, filing

Adjust schedule accordingly.

14. Tool Stack Rationalization Checklist

Use case: When evaluating software subscriptions for redundancy

Ask before renewing any tool:

  • Is this used by ≥80% of the team?
  • Does it solve a problem no other tool handles?
  • Can we automate or eliminate this workflow instead?
  • What’s the true cost (time + money)?

Example:

Tool: StatusHub Pro

Used by: 3 of 12 team members

Overlap with: Slack + Asana

Action: Cancel, migrate updates to #status channel

15. Outcome-Based To-Do List

Use case: When transforming task lists into goal-driven actions

Rewrite tasks as outcomes to increase clarity and motivation.

Instead of: “Schedule meeting”

Write: “Align marketing and sales on campaign launch (outcome: shared plan)”

Instead of: “Write report”

Write: “Deliver Q3 performance insights to leadership (outcome: budget approval)”

16. Weekly Intentions Statement

Use case: Monday morning to set focus and mindset

Write and read aloud a short intentions statement.

Example:

“This week, I focus on completing the client proposal, reducing meeting load by 20%, and protecting two 90-minute deep work blocks. I will say no to non-essential requests and communicate priorities clearly. My goal is progress, not perfection.”

17. Automation Opportunity Identifier

Use case: When reviewing repetitive tasks

Scan your task log for:

  • Tasks done ≥2x/week
  • Tasks requiring data transfer between apps
  • Templates you rewrite often

Then ask: “Can this be automated with templates, macros, or tools like Zapier?”

Example:

Task: “Weekly sales summary email”

Frequency: 5x/month

Steps: Pull data, format, send

Automation: Use Google Apps Script to auto-generate and email every Monday 8 AM

19. Task Delegation Script

Use case: When assigning work to ensure clarity and ownership

Use this script when delegating:

“[Name], I’d like you to own [task/project].

Objective: [desired outcome]

Deadline: [date/time]

Resources: [links, docs, contacts]

Success looks like: [specific, measurable result]

Check-ins: [frequency or milestone reviews]

Any questions or blockers?”

Example: “Maria, I’d like you to own the client onboarding checklist update. Objective: Reduce setup time by 20%. Deadline: Nov 10. Success looks like: 5+ process improvements documented and tested.”

20. End-of-Day Shutdown Ritual

Use case: Every workday to close tasks and reduce mental clutter

Perform a 5-minute shutdown ritual:

  1. Review today’s completed tasks – check off
  2. Move unfinished items to tomorrow’s plan
  3. Clear desktop and inbox (archive/delete)
  4. Write one win from today
  5. Set top priority for tomorrow

Example:

✅ Done: Team sync, proposal draft, expense report

📅 Moved: Finalize draft → Tomorrow 9 AM

🗑 Cleared: Desktop files, inbox (down to 12)

🏆 Win: Got client approval on scope

🎯 Tomorrow: Deliver final proposal by 11 AM

6. Morning Routine Booster

Use case: When starting a new project or task that requires high energy and focus

"Kickstart your day with a 10-minute morning routine that sets you up for success. Begin with 5 minutes of deep breathing exercises, followed by 3 minutes of stretching, and finish with 2 minutes of positive affirmations. This simple yet powerful routine will increase your energy levels, improve your mental clarity, and prepare you to tackle even the most challenging tasks."

7. Prioritization Framework

Use case: When faced with multiple tasks and limited time

"Use the Eisenhower Matrix to prioritize your tasks based on their urgency and importance. Divide your tasks into four quadrants: urgent & important, important but not urgent, urgent but not important, and not urgent or important. Focus on the most critical tasks first, and eliminate or delegate less important ones. This framework will help you manage your time more effectively and reduce stress."

8. Time Blocking Template

Use case: When scheduling tasks and appointments

"Use a time blocking template to schedule your tasks and appointments in fixed, uninterrupted blocks of time. Allocate specific time slots for each task, and leave breaks in between to recharge. This template will help you avoid multitasking, minimize distractions, and increase your productivity. Example: 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM: Task A, 10:30 AM - 10:45 AM: Break, 10:45 AM - 12:00 PM: Task B."

9. Goal Setting Worksheet

Use case: When setting new goals or reviewing existing ones

"Use a goal setting worksheet to clarify and achieve your objectives. Write down your goal, and then break it down into smaller, actionable steps. Identify the resources you need, potential obstacles, and a deadline for completion. This worksheet will help you create a clear plan, stay focused, and track your progress. Example: Goal - Increase productivity by 20% in the next 6 weeks. Step 1: Identify time-wasting activities, Step 2: Implement a time management system."

10. Focus-Enhancing Music Playlist

Use case: When working on tasks that require intense focus

"Create a focus-enhancing music playlist that helps you concentrate and stay productive. Choose instrumental music with a consistent beat and minimal lyrics, such as classical or lo-fi hip hop. Avoid music with sudden changes in tempo or volume, and opt for playlists with a calming atmosphere. This playlist will help you block out distractions, improve your focus, and maintain a steady workflow."

11. Task Automation Script

Use case: When performing repetitive tasks that can be automated

"Use a task automation script to streamline your workflow and save time. Identify tasks that can be automated, such as data entry or email sorting, and create a script that performs these tasks for you. This script will help you reduce manual labor, minimize errors, and increase your productivity. Example: A script that automatically sorts and responds to routine emails, freeing up time for more important tasks."

12. Meeting Agenda Template

Use case: When scheduling meetings or conferences

"Use a meeting agenda template to ensure productive and efficient meetings. Include the meeting objective, topics to be discussed, and a clear timeline. Allocate specific time slots for each topic, and leave room for questions and discussions. This template will help you stay on track, avoid unnecessary conversations, and achieve your meeting objectives. Example: Meeting Objective - Discuss project progress, Topic 1: Project updates (10 minutes), Topic 2: Challenges and solutions (20 minutes)."

13. Email Management System

Use case: When dealing with a high volume of emails

"Implement an email management system to reduce email overload and increase productivity. Use a combination of filters, labels, and folders to categorize and prioritize your emails. Set aside specific times to check and respond to emails, and avoid multitasking. This system will help you manage your inbox more efficiently, reduce stress, and stay focused on important tasks."

14. Break Reminder Notification

Use case: When working long hours without taking breaks

"Set up a break reminder notification to ensure you take regular breaks and maintain your productivity. Schedule reminders to take a break every hour, and use this time to stretch, move around, or practice relaxation techniques. This notification will help you avoid burnout, recharge your energy, and come back to your task with renewed focus and creativity."

15. Task Delegation Checklist

Use case: When delegating tasks to team members or colleagues

"Use a task delegation checklist to ensure clear communication and effective task assignment. Include the task description, deadlines, and required resources. Specify the expected outcome, and provide feedback mechanisms for the delegatee. This checklist will help you delegate tasks efficiently, avoid misunderstandings, and increase team productivity. Example: Task - Complete project report, Deadline - Friday, Resources - Project data and templates."

16. Time Tracking Spreadsheet

Use case: When monitoring time spent on tasks or projects

"Use a time tracking spreadsheet to monitor your time usage and optimize your productivity. Record the task, start time, end time, and duration. Analyze your time usage patterns, identify time-wasting activities, and adjust your schedule accordingly. This spreadsheet will help you stay focused, prioritize tasks, and achieve your goals more efficiently."

17. Productivity Journal Prompt

Use case: When reflecting on your productivity and progress

"Use a productivity journal prompt to reflect on your progress, identify areas for improvement, and set new goals. Ask yourself questions like 'What did I accomplish today?', 'What challenges did I face?', and 'What can I improve tomorrow?'. This prompt will help you develop a growth mindset, celebrate your achievements, and maintain a high level of productivity."

18. Goal Achievement Celebration Plan

Use case: When achieving a significant goal or milestone

"Create a goal achievement celebration plan to acknowledge and celebrate your accomplishments. Identify the goal, the achievement, and the desired celebration outcome. Choose a reward that motivates and inspires you, such as a fun activity or a gift. This plan will help you recognize your progress, reinforce positive habits, and stay motivated to achieve future goals. Example: Goal - Complete a marathon, Achievement - Finished the race in under 4 hours, Celebration - Treat myself to a weekend getaway."

19. Stress Reduction Technique

Use case: When feeling overwhelmed or stressed

"Use a stress reduction technique to calm your mind and regain focus. Practice deep breathing exercises, progressive muscle relaxation, or meditation. Take a short break, step away from your task, and engage in an activity that brings you joy and relaxation. This technique will help you manage stress, improve your mood, and maintain your productivity. Example: Take 5 minutes to practice deep breathing, inhaling for 4 seconds, holding for 4 seconds, and exhaling for 4 seconds."

20. Weekly Review Template

Use case: When reviewing progress and planning for the upcoming week

"Use a weekly review template to reflect on your progress, identify areas for improvement, and plan for the upcoming week. Include sections for accomplishments, challenges, and lessons learned. Set new goals and priorities, and create an action plan for the next week. This template will help you stay focused, adjust your strategy, and maintain a high level of productivity. Example: Weekly Review Template - Accomplishments: Completed project report, Challenges: Delayed feedback from team members, Lessons Learned: Improve communication channels, Next Week's Goals: Finalize project presentation."
