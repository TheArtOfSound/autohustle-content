18 Client Brief Templates for Freelance Content Creators

Updated: March 09, 2026

18 client brief templates for freelance content creators — streamline project intake for blogs, videos, podcasts, and social content.

What's Inside

Template 1: General Content Project Intake Form

When to use: For initial onboarding of new clients across any content type (blogs, videos, social, etc.) to gather essential project and brand details.

[CLIENT NAME]

[CLIENT COMPANY/BRAND]

[CONTACT EMAIL]

[PHONE NUMBER (OPTIONAL)]

[PROJECT START DATE]

[PROJECT DEADLINE]

Project Overview

What is the purpose of this content? [e.g., drive traffic, boost engagement, explain a product, grow email list]

Who is the primary audience? [e.g., small business owners, fitness beginners, SaaS developers]

What action should the audience take after consuming this content? [e.g., sign up, purchase, share, subscribe]

Content Format

Type of content: [Blog post / Video script / Podcast episode / Social media caption / Other: __________]

Preferred tone and style: [e.g., professional, conversational, humorous, authoritative]

Word count or runtime: [__________]

Brand Guidelines

Link to brand style guide (if available): [URL]

Keywords or SEO focus (if applicable): [__________]

Do you have preferred terminology or banned words? [List: __________]

Deliverables

Final deliverable(s): [e.g., 1,500-word blog post in Google Doc, 3 TikTok scripts in PDF]

File format and delivery method: [e.g., Google Drive link, email attachment, Dropbox]

Approvals & Revisions

Primary decision-maker: [NAME AND EMAIL]

Number of revision rounds included: [__________]

Preferred feedback method: [Email / Google Doc comments / Video call / Other: __________]

Additional Notes

Anything else I should know before starting?

[________________________________________________________]

Signature (client): ________________________ Date: __________

Sent by: [YOUR NAME], Freelance Content Creator

Template 2: Blog Post Brief

When to use: When a client requests a single blog post or article and you need clear direction on topic, keywords, and structure.

Client: [CLIENT NAME]

Project: Blog Post – [BLOG POST TITLE OR TOPIC]

Deadline: [DUE DATE]

Publish Date (if known): [PUBLISH DATE]

Objective

What goal should this blog post achieve? [e.g., rank for [KEYWORD], promote a new product, answer a common customer question]

Target Audience

Who are we writing for? [e.g., tech-savvy parents aged 30–45, startup founders, vegan travelers]

What do they already know? [__________]

What do they need to learn? [__________]

Content Requirements

Primary keyword: [__________]

Secondary keywords (optional): [__________]

Word count: [__________]

Tone: [e.g., informative, friendly, persuasive]

Structure Guidelines

Suggested outline:

  1. Introduction – [HOOK IDEA OR ANGLE]
  2. Section 1 – [TOPIC]
  3. Section 2 – [TOPIC]
  4. Section 3 – [TOPIC]
  5. Conclusion – [CALL TO ACTION OR SUMMARY]

Include:

[ ] Header images (provide or source?)

[ ] Internal links (list URLs): [__________]

[ ] External links (list URLs): [__________]

[ ] CTAs (e.g., “Download our guide,” “Book a call”): [__________]

Reference Materials

Existing blog posts you like: [URL, URL]

Competitor posts for inspiration: [URL, URL]

Other resources: [__________]

Approved by: [CLIENT NAME] Date: __________
