15 Email Templates for Freelance Writers to Win Repeat Clients

Updated: March 09, 2026

3 email templates designed to help freelance writers reconnect with past clients and secure repeat work — ready to personalize and send.

What's Inside

  • 15 proven email templates
  • focused on re-engagement and follow-ups
  • easy to customize

Template 1: Welcome & Onboarding Confirmation

When to use: Right after a client agrees to hire you — confirm project details, set expectations, and start the relationship professionally.

Hi [Client Name],

Thank you so much for choosing me to work on [Project Title]! I’m excited to collaborate with you and deliver high-quality content that meets your goals.

To ensure we’re aligned, here’s a quick summary of what we discussed:

  • Project Scope: [Brief description of deliverables, e.g., 2 blog posts (1,000 words each)]
  • Deadline: [Due date/time, including time zone if applicable]
  • Tone & Style: [e.g., Professional yet conversational, targeting small business owners]
  • Target Audience: [e.g., Entrepreneurs aged 30–45 looking to scale their startups]
  • Preferred Communication: [e.g., Email updates every 3 days; quick questions via Slack]

Please review this and let me know if anything needs adjusting. If all looks good, I’ll get started right away!

Looking forward to a great collaboration.

Best regards,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Job Title, e.g., Freelance Content Writer]

[Phone Number, optional]

Template 2: Mid-Project Progress Update

When to use: Midway through a project to reassure the client, show progress, and invite early feedback.

Hi [Client Name],

I wanted to give you a quick update on the [Project Title] we’re working on. I’m on track to meet our deadline of [Due Date], and I’ve completed [e.g., the first draft of both blog posts / research and outline for the whitepaper].

Here’s what’s done so far:

  • [Task completed, e.g., Topic research and keyword mapping]
  • [Task completed, e.g., First draft of Blog Post #1]

Next steps:

  • [e.g., Revise based on your feedback]
  • [e.g., Final edits and formatting]

If you’d like to review anything early—like an outline or draft—I’m happy to share it now. Just let me know!

Talk soon,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Website URL]

Template 3: Final Delivery with Value-Add Tips

When to use: When delivering completed work — include extra insights to increase perceived value and encourage repeat hiring.

Hi [Client Name],

Great news — your [Project Title], “[Deliverable Name],” is complete and attached here [or linked below].

I’ve crafted this with your goals in mind — focusing on [e.g., SEO optimization, reader engagement, brand voice consistency] — and I believe it will resonate well with your audience.

As a bonus, here are 3 quick tips to get more traction from this piece:

  1. [e.g., Share it in your newsletter with a compelling subject line like “X Ways to Solve [Pain Point]”]
  2. [e.g., Break up key points into social media graphics for LinkedIn/Twitter]
  3. [e.g., Add internal links to related content on your site to boost SEO]

Let me know if you’d like help adapting this content for other formats (e.g., email series, social posts). I’d love to support your next steps.

Looking forward to your thoughts!

Best,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Website URL]

Template 4: Polite Deadline Reminder (Gentle Nudge)

When to use: When a client is late in providing feedback or materials — keep it friendly and professional.

Hi [Client Name],

I hope you’re doing well! I’m following up on the [Project Title] — I’m ready to move forward with [next step, e.g., final edits], but I’m waiting on your feedback for [specific item, e.g., the draft from last week].

Just a gentle reminder so we can stay on track for our target publish date of [Date]. If you need more time or have questions, no problem — just let me know what works best for you.

I’m happy to jump on a quick call if that helps speed things up.

Thanks again,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Website URL]

Template 5: Post-Delivery Feedback Request

When to use: After delivering a project — ask for feedback to improve and open the door for future work.

Hi [Client Name],

I hope you had a chance to review the [Deliverable Name] I sent over for [Project Title]. I’d love to hear your thoughts!

Could you spare 2 minutes to share feedback on:

  • How well the content met your goals
  • What you’d like to see more (or less) of next time
  • Any improvements to our process

Your input helps me serve you better — especially if we work together again.

And if you’re happy with the results, I’d be honored if you considered me for your next project or referred me to a colleague.

Thanks again for the opportunity!

Warm regards,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Website URL]

When to use: 3–6 months after your last project — re-engage a past client who might need writing help again.

I wanted to check in and see how things are going on your end. Are you currently working on any content projects — like blog posts, case studies, or website updates — that could use a writer’s touch?

I’ve recently helped clients in [Your Niche, e.g., SaaS, health & wellness] with [e.g., SEO blog strategies, lead-generating email sequences], and I’d love to support you again if the need arises.

No pressure at all — just keeping the door open!

Warm regards,

Template 8: Offer a Content Audit or Refresh (Value-First Outreach)

When to use: To pitch repeat work by offering a free or low-cost audit of their existing content.

Hi [Client Name],

I hope you’re doing great! I was recently reviewing the [Deliverable Name] we created together for [Project Title], and it got me thinking — how’s the content performing these days?

Many of my clients find that older posts can be refreshed to boost traffic, improve SEO, or align with new brand messaging. As a quick favor, I’d love to offer you a free content audit of that piece (or any other page/post you’d like to update).

I’ll provide:

  • 3 actionable suggestions to improve performance
  • Ideas for updating stats, tone, or structure
  • Optional: A quote to refresh the content if you’d like

No obligation — just a way I can add value and stay connected. Let me know if you’re interested!

Best,

[Your Full Name]

[Your Website URL]
