15 Client Onboarding Email Templates for Freelance Designers

Updated: March 05, 2026

15 client onboarding email templates for freelance designers — ready to use for discovery calls, contract follow-ups, and project kickoffs.

What's Inside

  • 15 professionally written email templates
  • customizable for different design specialties
  • saves hours in client setup

Template 1: Initial Outreach

When to use: When you first make contact with a potential client after they've expressed interest in your services.

Subject: Excited to Discuss Your Design Needs, [CLIENT NAME]

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

I hope this email finds you well! I came across your interest in my design services and wanted to reach out personally.

I'm [YOUR NAME], a [YOUR DESIGN SPECIALTY] designer with [NUMBER] years of experience. I've helped businesses like [SIMILAR CLIENT] achieve [SPECIFIC RESULT], and I'm confident I can do the same for you.

I'd love to hop on a quick call to learn more about your project and see if we'd be a great fit. Are you available for a discovery call next [WEEK/DAY]? I'm available [LIST TIME SLOTS].

Looking forward to connecting!

Best,

[YOUR NAME]

[YOUR CONTACT INFO]

Template 2: Discovery Call Confirmation

When to use: After a client agrees to a discovery call, to confirm the details.

Subject: Confirming Our Discovery Call on [DATE] at [TIME]

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

Great news! I've got [DATE] at [TIME] blocked off in my calendar for our discovery call. I'm really looking forward to learning more about your project and seeing how I can help.

To make the most of our time together, could you please send me any relevant materials or briefs you have beforehand? That way, I can come prepared with some initial ideas.

Here's a link to join the call: [VIDEO CALL LINK]

If you need to reschedule, just let me know, and I'll do my best to accommodate.

Talk soon,

[YOUR NAME]

[YOUR CONTACT INFO]

Template 4: Contract & Invoice

When to use: When sending the contract and invoice after the client agrees to move forward.

Subject: Contract & Invoice for [PROJECT NAME]

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

I'm thrilled to officially start working on [PROJECT NAME]! As discussed, please find attached:

  • Contract outlining our agreement
  • Invoice for the initial deposit ([DEPOSIT AMOUNT])

Please review the contract at your earliest convenience and let me know if you have any questions. Once signed, you can send it back to me via email or [PREFERRED SIGNING TOOL].

The invoice is due by [DUE DATE]. You can make the payment via [PAYMENT METHOD].

Once I receive the signed contract and initial deposit, we can officially kick off the project!

Looking forward to creating something amazing together.

Best,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 4: Introduction and Project Kickoff

When to use: After a client has signed a contract and paid the initial deposit, to introduce yourself and kickoff the project.

Dear [CLIENT NAME],

I hope this email finds you well. I wanted to personally reach out and welcome you to [YOUR COMPANY NAME]. We're excited to start working on your [PROJECT TYPE] project, and I'm looking forward to collaborating with you to bring your vision to life.

As discussed, our project timeline is [PROJECT TIMELINE], and we'll be working closely together to ensure that your needs are met. If you have any questions or concerns, please don't hesitate to reach out.

Next steps: I'll be sending over a detailed project schedule and a list of tasks that we'll need to complete to ensure the project's success. In the meantime, if you could please provide me with [SPECIFIC INFORMATION NEEDED], that would be great.

Looking forward to a successful project!

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

[YOUR NAME]

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]
