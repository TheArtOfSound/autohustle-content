Template 1: Video Content Brief

When to use: When creating short-form or long-form video content for a client, including social media videos, YouTube explainers, or promotional clips.

Project Title: [Video Topic or Title]

Client Name: [Client Full Name or Brand]

Content Creator: [Your Name]

Due Date: [Delivery Deadline]

Publishing Platform(s): [e.g., Instagram Reels, YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn]

Objective:

Create an engaging video that [e.g., introduces a new product, explains a service, boosts brand awareness, drives traffic to a landing page].

Target Audience:

  • Age Range: [e.g., 25–40]
  • Interests: [e.g., fitness, personal finance, skincare]
  • Pain Points: [e.g., lack of time, budget constraints, confusion about product]
  • Where They Spend Time Online: [e.g., Instagram, Reddit, Substack]

Video Type:

[Choose one: Explainer, Product Demo, Testimonial Highlight, Behind-the-Scenes, Educational, Promotional]

Length: [e.g., 60 seconds, 3 minutes]

Tone & Style: [e.g., professional yet friendly, upbeat and energetic, minimalist and calm]

Key Message:

[Core takeaway you want viewers to remember, e.g., "Our app saves busy professionals 5 hours per week."]

Script Notes:

  • Must include: [e.g., CTA to visit website, mention of limited-time offer]
  • Avoid: [e.g., jargon, specific competitor names]
  • Brand voice guidelines: [e.g., use contractions, avoid passive voice]

Visual Direction:

  • Preferred color palette: [e.g., brand colors: #2A5C82, #F2C94C]
  • Footage needed: [e.g., B-roll of people working, screen recordings, animation]
  • Text overlays: [e.g., key stats, quotes, CTA]
  • Music: [e.g., upbeat acoustic, no vocals]

Call to Action (CTA):

[What should viewers do next? e.g., “Visit [Website URL] to get started” or “Follow us for more tips.”]

Assets Provided by Client:

  • [ ] Logo files
  • [ ] Product images
  • [ ] Brand guidelines
  • [ ] Script draft
  • [ ] Footage or clips

Notes:

[Any additional information, e.g., “Client will approve storyboard before filming,” or “Include subtitles for accessibility.”]

Template 5: YouTube Video Brief

When to use: For planning and scripting longer-form YouTube content such as tutorials, vlogs, or thought leadership videos.

Video Title (Working): [e.g., “How I Grew My Channel to 10K Subscribers”]

Client/Channel Name: [e.g., [Client Name]’s Creative Lab]

Content Creator: [Your Name]

Publish Date: [Scheduled Upload Date]

Target Length: [e.g., 8–10 minutes]

Objective:

[Primary goal: e.g., grow subscribers, establish authority, promote a course]

Target Viewer:

  • Who they are: [e.g., aspiring YouTubers, solopreneurs, creatives]
  • What they struggle with: [e.g., consistency, editing, finding their niche]
  • What they want: [e.g., actionable steps, inspiration, proven systems]

Video Format:

[Choose: Tutorial, Storytelling, Review, Q&A, Listicle, Vlog]

Structure:

  1. Hook (0:00–0:30): [e.g., “I almost quit YouTube last year. Here’s what changed.”]
  2. Intro (0:30–1:00): [e.g., introduce topic and what viewer will learn]
  3. Main Content (1:00–7:30): [Break into 3–5 key points]
  4. CTA & Outro (7:30–end): [e.g., “Like, subscribe, and grab my free planner”]

Tone & Style:

[e.g., authentic and vulnerable, energetic and fast-paced, calm and instructional]

Key Talking Points:

  • [Point 1: e.g., “Start with repurposing content”]
  • [Point 2: e.g., “Batch your filming”]
  • [Point 3: e.g., “Engage in the first 60 seconds”]

On-Screen Elements:

  • Lower thirds: [e.g., name/title on screen]
  • B-roll: [e.g., footage of editing, filming, analytics dashboard]
  • Text overlays: [e.g., emphasize key stats]
  • End screen: [e.g., promote another video and subscribe button]

Call to Action:

[What to ask viewers to do: e.g., “Download the free YouTube checklist at [URL]”]

Assets Provided by Client:

  • [ ] Script draft
  • [ ] B-roll footage
  • [ ] Thumbnail concept
  • [ ] Brand audio sting

Notes:

[e.g., “Client prefers natural lighting,” or “Include closed captions in final export.”]

  • Who they are: [e.g., solopreneurs, coaches, creators]
  • Where they are in the journey: [e.g., just starting out, scaling their offer]

Hook (First 3 Seconds):

[Critical attention-grabber: e.g., “Stop wasting time on content that doesn’t convert.”]

Narrative Flow:

  1. Hook: [Repeat above]
  2. Problem: [e.g., “You’re posting daily but not growing.”]
  3. Solution: [e.g., “Try these 3 underused formats.”]
  4. Examples: [List 3 quick ideas with visuals]

Tone & Style:

[e.g., direct and punchy, fun and relatable, confident and professional]

Visual Style:

  • Primary format: [e.g., talking head with text overlays, screen recording, B-roll montage]
  • Text size/font: [e.g., large sans-serif, easy to read on mobile]
  • Transitions: [e.g., quick cuts, zoom effects]
  • Music: [e.g., trending audio from Instagram library, upbeat pop]

Caption (Draft):

[Write a sample caption: e.g., “Swipe ➡️ for 3 content ideas that actually work. Save this to try later!”]

Hashtags:

  • [e.g., #ContentCreatorTips #SolopreneurLife #ReelsStrategy]

Call to Action:

[On-screen and audio: e.g., “Follow for daily creator tips” or “DM ‘IDEAS’ for the full list”]

Assets Provided by Client:

  • [ ] Trending audio link
  • [ ] Brand fonts
  • [ ] Product images
  • [ ] Previous high-performing Reel

Notes:

[e.g., “Use vertical 9:16 ratio,” or “Client will approve storyboard before filming.”]
