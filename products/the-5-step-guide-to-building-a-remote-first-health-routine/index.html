The 5-Step Guide to Building a Remote-First Health Routine

As a remote worker or digital nomad, you're likely no stranger to the freedom and flexibility that comes with working from anywhere. However, this lifestyle can also make it challenging to maintain consistent health habits. Without a traditional office environment, it's easy to fall into unhealthy patterns, such as skipping meals, neglecting exercise, and working long hours without taking breaks. But with a little planning and intention, you can build a remote-first health routine that supports your physical and mental well-being.

Introduction to Remote-First Health

Remote-first health refers to the intentional prioritization of health and wellness in a remote work environment. It's about recognizing that your health is just as important as your work, and making conscious choices to support your overall well-being. This might involve creating a dedicated workspace that promotes good posture and reduces eye strain, establishing a daily routine that includes time for exercise and self-care, or seeking out community and connection with fellow remote workers.

One of the biggest challenges of remote work is the lack of structure and accountability. Without a traditional office environment, it's easy to fall into bad habits, such as working too much or too little, or neglecting to take care of your physical and mental health. But by prioritizing remote-first health, you can create a sense of structure and routine that supports your well-being, even when you're working from a coffee shop or co-working space.

So why is remote-first health so important? For one, it can help you maintain your physical health, even when you're not working in a traditional office environment. Regular exercise, healthy eating, and adequate sleep are all essential for maintaining your physical health, and can help you stay energized and focused throughout the day. Remote-first health can also support your mental health, by providing a sense of structure and routine, and helping you stay connected with others.

In this guide, we'll walk you through the 5 steps to building a remote-first health routine. From setting health goals and routines, to managing stress and tracking progress, we'll provide you with the practical tips and actionable takeaways you need to prioritize your health and wellness as a remote worker.

Step 1: Setting Health Goals and Routines

The first step in building a remote-first health routine is to set health goals and routines. This involves identifying what you want to achieve in terms of your physical and mental health, and creating a plan for how you'll get there. Start by asking yourself some questions:

  • What are my health goals? Do I want to lose weight, increase my energy levels, or improve my overall sense of well-being?
  • What are my biggest health challenges? Do I struggle with motivation, or do I have a hard time sticking to a routine?
  • What kind of routine do I want to establish? Do I want to wake up early and start my day with a workout, or do I prefer to work out in the evening?

Once you have a sense of what you want to achieve, you can start setting specific, measurable, and achievable health goals. For example, you might set a goal to:

  • Exercise for 30 minutes, 3 times per week
  • Eat 5 servings of fruits and vegetables per day
  • Get 7-8 hours of sleep per night
  • Practice meditation or deep breathing for 10 minutes per day

Next, you'll want to create a routine that supports your health goals. This might involve:

  • Waking up at the same time every day, and starting your day with a consistent routine
  • Scheduling time for exercise, self-care, and relaxation
  • Creating a meal plan or grocery list to support healthy eating
  • Setting reminders or notifications to help you stay on track

For example, let's say you want to establish a morning routine that includes exercise, meditation, and a healthy breakfast. You might set your alarm for 6:00 am, and then follow this routine:

  • 6:00 am: Wake up, stretch, and drink a glass of water
  • 6:15 am: Go for a 30-minute walk or jog
  • 6:45 am: Practice meditation or deep breathing for 10 minutes
  • 7:00 am: Eat a healthy breakfast, such as oatmeal with fruit and nuts
  • 7:30 am: Start your workday, feeling energized and focused

Remember, the key is to create a routine that works for you, and that you can stick to in the long term. Don't be too hard on yourself if you miss a day or two - simply get back on track, and keep moving forward.

Step 2: Nutrition and Meal Planning for Remote Workers

As a remote worker, it can be tempting to rely on convenience foods or takeout, especially when you're working from home or on the road. But a healthy diet is essential for maintaining your physical and mental health, and can help you stay energized and focused throughout the day.

So how can you prioritize nutrition and meal planning as a remote worker? Here are some tips:

  • Plan your meals in advance: Take some time each week to plan out your meals, and make a grocery list to support healthy eating. You can find healthy recipes online, or use a meal planning app to help you stay organized.
  • Keep healthy snacks!: Keep a stash of healthy snacks, such as nuts, fruits, and carrot sticks, to munch on throughout the day.
  • Cook at home: Try to cook at home as much as possible, using fresh ingredients and healthy cooking methods. This can help you save money, and ensure that you're getting the nutrients you need.
  • Avoid sugary drinks: Sugary drinks, such as soda and juice, can be a major source of empty calories, and can wreak havoc on your health. Try to stick to water, unsweetened tea, or black coffee instead.
  • Take breaks to eat: When you're working remotely, it can be tempting to eat at your desk, or skip meals altogether. But taking breaks to eat can help you stay focused, and give you a chance to rest and recharge.

For example, let's say you're working from a coffee shop, and you want to prioritize healthy eating. You might pack a lunch, such as a salad or a sandwich, and bring some healthy snacks, such as fruit or energy bars. You could also take breaks to grab a healthy snack, or meet a friend for a walk and a meal.

Some healthy meal ideas for remote workers include:

  • Overnight oats: Mix together rolled oats, milk, and your choice of nuts, seeds, or fruit, and refrigerate overnight. In the morning, you'll have a healthy and delicious breakfast ready to go.
  • Salads in a jar: Layer ingredients, such as greens, vegetables, and proteins, in a jar, and top with a healthy dressing. This makes for a quick and easy lunch, and can be easily customized to your tastes.
  • Soup and sandwich: Cook up a big batch of soup, such as lentil or vegetable soup, and pair it with a healthy sandwich, such as a whole grain wrap with turkey and avocado.

Remember, the key is to prioritize whole, unprocessed foods, and to stay hydrated throughout the day. By planning your meals in advance, and keeping healthy snacks on hand, you can stay energized and focused, even when you're working remotely.

Step 3: Staying Active While Working Remotely

Regular exercise is essential for maintaining your physical and mental health, and can help you stay energized and focused throughout the day. But as a remote worker, it can be challenging to stay active, especially if you're working from home or spending long hours at your desk.

So how can you prioritize physical activity as a remote worker? Here are some tips:

  • Schedule it in: Treat exercise as a non-negotiable part of your daily routine, and schedule it in your calendar. This might mean waking up early to go for a run, or taking a break to do a quick workout at home.
  • Find activities you enjoy: Experiment with different types of exercise, such as yoga, hiking, or swimming, to find what you enjoy and what keeps you motivated.
  • Invest in a standing desk: A standing desk can help you stay active and engaged, even when you're working at your desk. You can also try incorporating stretching or movement into your workday, such as by doing a few jumping jacks or stretching exercises.
  • Take breaks to move: When you're working remotely, it can be tempting to sit for long hours without taking a break. But taking breaks to move can help you stay focused, and give you a chance to rest and recharge.
  • Find a workout buddy: Having a workout buddy can be a great motivator, and can help you stay accountable. You might find a friend or colleague who shares your interest in exercise, or join a fitness class or group.

For example, let's say you're working from home, and you want to prioritize physical activity. You might start your day with a 30-minute yoga practice, or take a break to go for a walk or do a quick workout. You could also invest in a standing desk, or try incorporating stretching or movement into your workday.

Some fun and creative ways to stay active as a remote worker include:

  • Taking a walking meeting: Instead of sitting at your desk, try taking a walking meeting with a colleague or friend. This can be a great way to get some exercise, and can help you stay energized and focused.
  • Doing a home workout: There are many free workout videos and apps available online, such as YouTube Fitness or Nike Training Club. You can also invest in a few pieces of equipment, such as a yoga mat or a set of dumbbells, to help you stay active at home.
  • Trying a new outdoor activity: If you're working remotely, you might have the freedom to try new outdoor activities, such as hiking, kayaking, or rock climbing. This can be a great way to stay active, and can help you explore new places and try new things.

Remember, the key is to find activities that you enjoy, and that keep you motivated. By prioritizing physical activity, and incorporating movement into your daily routine, you can stay energized and focused, even when you're working remotely.

Step 4: Managing Stress and Mental Health

As a remote worker, you may face unique challenges when it comes to managing stress and mental health. Without a traditional office environment, it can be easy to feel isolated or disconnected from others, and the lack of structure and accountability can take a toll on your mental well-being.

So how can you prioritize mental health and stress management as a remote worker? Here are some tips:

  • Stay connected with others: Make an effort to stay connected with friends, family, and colleagues, whether it's through video calls, messaging apps, or social media. This can help you feel less isolated, and can provide a sense of community and support.
  • Prioritize self-care: Make time for activities that bring you joy and relaxation, such as reading, meditation, or spending time in nature. This can help you manage stress, and can provide a sense of calm and well-being.
  • Set boundaries: Establish clear boundaries between your work and personal life, and prioritize time for rest and relaxation. This can help you avoid burnout, and can provide a sense of balance and harmony.
  • Seek out support: If you're struggling with mental health issues, such as anxiety or depression, don't be afraid to seek out support. This might mean talking to a therapist, joining a support group, or reaching out to a trusted friend or family member.
  • Practice mindfulness: Mindfulness practices, such as meditation or deep breathing, can help you stay present and focused, and can provide a sense of calm and well-being.

For example, let's say you're working from home, and you're feeling overwhelmed and stressed. You might take a break to practice some deep breathing exercises, or go for a walk outside to clear your head. You could also schedule a video call with a friend or colleague, or make time for a relaxing activity, such as reading or listening to music.

Some additional resources for managing stress and mental health as a remote worker include:

  • Mental health apps: There are many mental health apps available, such as Headspace or Calm, that can provide guided meditation and stress management techniques.
  • Online support groups: Online support groups, such as Remote.co or Nomad List, can provide a sense of community and connection with other remote workers.
  • Therapy or counseling: If you're struggling with mental health issues, consider seeking out therapy or counseling. This can provide a safe and supportive space to talk through your feelings and develop coping strategies.

Remember, prioritizing mental health and stress management is essential for maintaining your overall well-being as a remote worker. By staying connected with others, prioritizing self-care, and seeking out support when needed, you can manage stress and stay healthy, even when you're working remotely.

Step 5: Tracking Progress and Maintaining Motivation

The final step in building a remote-first health routine is to track your progress and maintain motivation. This involves monitoring your progress, celebrating your successes, and making adjustments as needed.

So how can you track your progress and maintain motivation as a remote worker? Here are some tips:

  • Use a habit tracker: A habit tracker can help you monitor your progress, and provide a sense of accountability. You can use a physical habit tracker, such as a planner or a spreadsheet, or download a habit tracking app, such as Habitica or HabitBull.
  • Set reminders: Set reminders or notifications to help you stay on track, and to remind you to take breaks or practice self-care.
  • Celebrate your successes: Celebrate your successes, no matter how small they may seem. This can help you stay motivated, and provide a sense of accomplishment and pride.
  • Make adjustments as needed: Don't be afraid to make adjustments as needed. If you're struggling to stick to your routine, or if you're not seeing the results you want, try making some changes, such as adjusting your schedule or trying a new activity.
  • Find a accountability partner: Find a friend or colleague who shares your health goals, and ask them to be your accountability partner. This can provide an added motivation, and can help you stay on track.

For example, let's say you're working on building a morning routine that includes exercise, meditation, and a healthy breakfast. You might use a habit tracker to monitor your progress, and set reminders to help you stay on track. You could also celebrate your successes, such as by treating yourself to a favorite meal or activity, and make adjustments as needed, such as by trying a new workout routine or adjusting your schedule.

Some additional tools for tracking progress and maintaining motivation include:

  • Fitness trackers: Fitness trackers, such as Fitbit or Garmin, can provide a detailed picture of your physical activity, and can help you stay motivated to move more.
  • Health apps: Health apps, such as MyFitnessPal or Lose It!, can help you track your nutrition, and provide a sense of accountability.
  • Journaling: Journaling can be a great way to reflect on your progress, and to identify areas for improvement. Try writing down your thoughts, feelings, and goals, and reflecting on your progress over time.

Remember, building a remote-first health routine takes time and effort, but it's worth it in the long run. By tracking your progress, maintaining motivation, and making adjustments as needed, you can stay healthy, happy, and productive, even when you're working remotely.
