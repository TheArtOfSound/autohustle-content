18 Client Brief Templates for Freelance Designers

Updated: March 07, 2026

18 client brief templates designed specifically for freelance designers to clarify project needs and reduce back-and-forth.

What's Inside

  • 18 customizable brief templates
  • covers logos, websites, branding, and more
  • structured to gather key requirements upfront

Template 1: Logo Design Discovery Brief

When to use: When starting a new logo design project and need to understand the client’s brand identity, preferences, and usage requirements.

Client Name: [CLIENT NAME]

Project Title: [PROJECT TITLE – e.g., “Logo Redesign for Urban Brew Cafe”]

Date Submitted: [DATE]

1. Company Overview

  • Company Name: [COMPANY NAME]
  • Industry: [INDUSTRY – e.g., Food & Beverage, Tech, Wellness]
  • Brief Description of What You Do: [DESCRIPTION – 2-3 sentences about your products/services]
  • Website (if applicable): [WEBSITE URL]

2. Brand Identity

  • Core Values: [VALUE 1, VALUE 2, VALUE 3 – e.g., Sustainability, Innovation, Simplicity]
  • Personality Traits (choose 3): [e.g., Modern, Trustworthy, Playful, Sophisticated, Bold]
  • Who Should Your Logo Appeal To? [TARGET AUDIENCE – e.g., Young professionals aged 25–35 who value eco-conscious brands]

3. Logo Preferences

  • Preferred Style: [e.g., Minimalist, Vintage, Hand-Drawn, Wordmark, Lettermark, Mascot]
  • Colors You Like (and dislike): [FAVORITE COLORS]; [COLORS TO AVOID]
  • Fonts You Admire: [e.g., Sans-serif, Serif, Rounded, Bold Typography]
  • Examples of Logos You Love: [LINK 1, LINK 2, LINK 3]
  • Examples of Logos You Dislike: [LINK 4, LINK 5]

4. Usage & Deliverables

  • Where Will the Logo Be Used? [e.g., Website, Business Cards, Packaging, Social Media]
  • Required File Formats: [e.g., PNG, JPG, SVG, EPS, PDF]
  • Need a Favicon Version? [Yes / No]

5. Timeline & Budget

  • Desired Completion Date: [DATE]
  • Approximate Budget Range: [BUDGET RANGE]

Template 2: Brand Identity Package Brief

When to use: For comprehensive branding projects that include logo, color palette, typography, and brand guidelines.

Client Name: [CLIENT NAME]

Project Title: [PROJECT TITLE – e.g., “Complete Branding for TerraHome Interiors”]

Date Submitted: [DATE]

1. Business Context

  • Company Name: [COMPANY NAME]
  • Tagline or Slogan (if any): [TAGLINE]
  • Mission Statement: [MISSION STATEMENT]
  • Target Audience: [DEMOGRAPHIC + PSYCHOGRAPHIC DETAILS – e.g., Eco-conscious homeowners, 30–50, urban suburbs]

2. Brand Personality

  • If Your Brand Were a Person, They Would Be: [DESCRIPTION – e.g., Calm, confident, well-dressed, approachable]
  • 3 Words That Describe Your Ideal Brand Tone: [WORD 1, WORD 2, WORD 3]
  • Competitors You Admire: [COMPETITOR 1, COMPETITOR 2]

3. Visual Direction

  • Preferred Aesthetic: [e.g., Scandinavian, Industrial, Bohemian, Corporate Luxury]
  • Color Emotions: [e.g., Trust, Energy, Calm, Luxury]
  • Typography Preferences: [e.g., Clean sans-serif, Elegant serif, Modern geometric]
  • Reference Images: [LINK TO INSPIRATION BOARD OR ATTACHMENTS]

4. Deliverables

Please confirm which items you’d like included:

☐ Logo (Primary + Variations)

☐ Color Palette (Primary & Secondary)

☐ Typography System (Headings, Body, Accent)

☐ Brand Guidelines PDF (10–15 pages)

☐ Business Card Design

☐ Social Media Profile Kit

☐ Other: [SPECIFY]

5. Timeline & Approval

  • Project Kickoff Date: [DATE]
  • Expected Delivery Date: [DATE]
  • Main Point of Contact: [NAME, EMAIL, PHONE]

Template 3: Website Redesign Intake Form

When to use: When a client wants to refresh or rebuild their existing website with new design and UX improvements.

Client Name: [CLIENT NAME]

Project Title: [PROJECT TITLE – e.g., “Website Redesign for Nova Fitness Studio”]

Current Website URL: [CURRENT URL]

Date Submitted: [DATE]

1. Project Goals

  • Why Are You Redesigning Your Website? [e.g., Outdated look, poor mobile performance, low conversion rates]
  • Top 3 Objectives for the New Site:
  1. [OBJECTIVE 1 – e.g., Increase class sign-ups by 30%]
  2. [OBJECTIVE 2 – e.g., Improve mobile user experience]
  3. [OBJECTIVE 3 – e.g., Showcase new service offerings]

2. Target Audience

  • Who Is Your Ideal Visitor? [DETAILED DESCRIPTION]
  • What Should They Do on Your Site? [e.g., Book a consultation, join mailing list, buy a product]

3. Design & UX Preferences

  • Websites You Admire (and Why): [LINK 1 – REASON, LINK 2 – REASON]
  • Must-Have Pages: [e.g., Home, About, Services, Testimonials, Contact]
  • Features Needed:

☐ Mobile-Responsive Design

☐ Contact Form

☐ Image Gallery

☐ Blog Section

☐ Booking/Checkout System

☐ Other: [SPECIFY]

4. Content & Assets

  • Will You Provide Copy? [Yes / No – If no, do you need copywriting support?]
  • Will You Provide Images? [Yes / No – If no, do you need stock photo curation?]
  • CMS Preference: [e.g., WordPress, Webflow, Shopify, Custom]

5. Timeline & Handoff

  • Desired Launch Date: [DATE]
  • Technical Contact (if applicable): [NAME, EMAIL]
  • Hosting Platform: [PLATFORM NAME]

Template 4: Social Media Graphics Starter Brief

When to use: When creating recurring social media visuals (posts, stories, ads) for a brand.

Client Name: [CLIENT NAME]

Project Title: [PROJECT TITLE – e.g., “Monthly Social Media Kit for Bloom Skincare”]

Date Submitted: [DATE]

1. Brand Overview

  • Company Name: [COMPANY NAME]
  • Primary Social Platforms: [e.g., Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, Facebook]
  • Target Audience: [AUDIENCE DESCRIPTION]
  • Brand Voice: [e.g., Friendly, Witty, Professional, Inspirational]

2. Content Themes

List 3–5 core content categories:

  1. [THEME 1 – e.g., Skincare Tips]
  2. [THEME 2 – e.g., Behind-the-Scenes]
  3. [THEME 3 – e.g., User Testimonials]
  4. [THEME 4 – e.g., Promotions]

3. Design Requirements

  • Preferred Dimensions: [e.g., 1080x1080, 1080x1920 for Stories]
  • Color Palette (or attach): [PRIMARY COLOR, SECONDARY COLOR, ACCENT COLOR]
  • Fonts to Use: [FONT NAME FOR HEADLINES, FONT NAME FOR BODY TEXT]
  • Logo Placement Preference: [e.g., Bottom right, Watermark, Not visible]

4. Content & Approval

  • Will You Provide Weekly Copy? [Yes / No]
  • Approval Process: [e.g., Review via email, Google Drive comments, Slack]
  • Turnaround Time for Feedback: [e.g., Within 48 hours]

5. Volume & Schedule

  • Number of Graphics Per Week/Month: [NUMBER]
  • First Batch Due: [DATE]
  • Ongoing Work? [Yes – Monthly Retainer / No – One-Time Project]

Template 5: Packaging Design Project Brief

When to use: For designing product packaging that aligns with brand identity and shelf appeal.

Client Name: [CLIENT NAME]

Project Title: [PROJECT TITLE – e.g., “New Packaging for Sierra Snacks Bars”]

Date Submitted: [DATE]

1. Product Details

  • Product Name: [PRODUCT NAME]
  • Product Type: [e.g., Organic Energy Bar, Skincare Serum, Candle]
  • Key Features/Benefits:
  1. [BENEFIT 1 – e.g., Vegan, Gluten-Free]
  2. [BENEFIT 2 – e.g., Eco-Friendly Packaging]
  3. [BENEFIT 3 – e.g., Made with Local Ingredients]

2. Target Market

  • Primary Customer: [DEMOGRAPHIC – e.g., Health-conscious women, 25–40]
  • Where Do They Shop? [e.g., Whole Foods, Online, Specialty Stores]
  • What Influences Their Purchase? [e.g., Clean design, sustainability claims, flavor variety]

3. Design Direction

  • Desired Mood: [e.g., Fresh, Luxurious, Playful, Minimalist]
  • Colors to Include: [COLORS]
  • Colors to Avoid: [COLORS]
  • Preferred Typography Style: [e.g., Handwritten, Bold Caps, Elegant Serif]
  • Reference Packaging Examples: [LINK 1, LINK 2, LINK 3]

4. Technical Requirements

  • Packaging Format: [e.g., Box, Pouch, Bottle, Tube]
  • Dimensions: [WIDTH x HEIGHT x DEPTH]
  • Print Requirements: [e.g., CMYK, Spot Colors, Matte Finish]
  • Legal/Regulatory Text Needed: [e.g., Ingredients, Net Weight, Allergens]

5. Timeline & Deliverables

  • First Draft Due: [DATE]
  • Final Artwork Delivery: [DATE]
  • File Format Needed: [e.g., Print-Ready PDF, AI, EPS]

Template 6: UI/UX App Design Kickoff Brief

When to use: When beginning a mobile or web app design project from concept to prototype.

Client Name: [CLIENT NAME]

Project Title: [PROJECT TITLE – e.g., “UI/UX Design for MedTrack Health App”]

Date Submitted: [DATE]

1. App Overview

  • App Name: [APP NAME]
  • Purpose of the App: [e.g., Help users track medication and receive reminders]
  • Target Users: [USER PROFILE – e.g., Elderly patients managing chronic conditions]
  • Platforms: ☐ iOS ☐ Android ☐ Web App

2. Key Features

List core user flows:

  1. [FEATURE 1 – e.g., User Registration & Profile Setup]
  2. [FEATURE 2 – e.g., Medication Logging]
  3. [FEATURE 3 – e.g., Push Notifications]
  4. [FEATURE 4 – e.g., Doctor Sharing Portal]

3. User Experience Goals

  • What Should Users Feel When Using the App? [e.g., Secure, Empowered, Calm]
  • Biggest User Pain Points to Solve: [e.g., Complex interfaces, missed doses]
  • Accessibility Needs: [e.g., Large text, screen reader compatibility]

4. Design Preferences

  • Style Reference Apps: [APP NAME – REASON, e.g., “Headspace – Calm, intuitive interface”]
  • Preferred Color Palette: [PRIMARY, SECONDARY, ACCENT]
  • Typography System: [FONT FAMILY FOR UI ELEMENTS]
  • Logo & Brand Assets Provided? [Yes / No – If yes, attach]

5. Deliverables & Timeline

  • Deliverables:

☐ User Flow Diagrams

☐ Wireframes (Low/High Fidelity)

☐ Interactive Prototype (Figma/Adobe XD)

☐ Design System (Optional)

  • Target Launch (Design Phase): [DATE]
  • Stakeholder Review Process: [e.g., Weekly syncs, feedback via Figma comments]

Template 7: Print Collateral Design Request

When to use: When designing physical marketing materials like brochures, flyers, or posters.

Client Name: [CLIENT NAME]

Project Title: [PROJECT TITLE – e.g., “Event Flyer & Brochure for ArtFest 2024”]

Date Submitted: [DATE]

1. Project Type

  • Material(s) Needed:

☐ Flyer

☐ Brochure (Fold: [e.g., Tri-fold, Bi-fold])

☐ Poster

☐ Business Cards

☐ Other: [SPECIFY]

2. Event or Campaign Details

  • Title: [EVENT/CAMPAIGN NAME]
  • Date & Time: [DATE, TIME]
  • Location: [VENUE NAME, ADDRESS]
  • Brief Description: [1–2 sentences about the event or offer]
  • Call to Action: [e.g., “Register Now,” “Visit Our Booth,” “Scan QR Code”]

3. Design Direction

  • Preferred Style: [e.g., Elegant, Bold, Retro, Modern Minimalist]
  • Brand Colors: [PRIMARY COLOR, SECONDARY COLOR]
  • Fonts to Use: [HEADING FONT, BODY FONT]
  • Images/Photos Provided? [Yes / No – If no, describe needed imagery]
  • Must-Include Elements:
  • Logo: [Yes / No]
  • QR Code: [Yes / No – If yes, link: [URL]]
  • Social Media Handles: [HANDLES]

4. Print Specifications

  • Dimensions: [e.g., 8.5 x 11 inches, A5, A4]
  • Paper Type/Finish: [e.g., Glossy, Matte, Recycled]
  • Print Quantity: [NUMBER]
  • Printer: [CLIENT’S PRINTER OR “Designer to Advise”]

5. Timeline

  • First Draft Due: [DATE]
  • Final Files for Print: [DATE]
  • Distribution Date: [DATE]
