25 Checklist Items for Small Business Tax Preparation

Updated: March 04, 2026

25 checklist items for small business tax preparation — covers documentation, deductions, and deadlines.

What's Inside

  • 25 essential tasks
  • organized by priority
  • easy to follow
  • [ ] Item 1: Confirm your business legal structure (sole proprietorship, LLC, S-Corp, etc.) to determine correct tax forms
  • [ ] Item 2: Verify your Employer Identification Number (EIN) or Social Security Number is on file with the IRS
  • [ ] Item 3: Ensure your business name, address, and contact information are up to date with the IRS and state agencies
  • [ ] Item 4: Gather all 1099-NEC forms received from clients for freelance or contract work
  • [ ] Item 5: Collect all 1099-K forms from payment processors like PayPal or Square
  • [ ] Item 6: Compile bank and credit card statements for all business accounts for the tax year
  • [ ] Item 7: Reconcile your accounting records (e.g., QuickBooks, Xero) with bank statements
  • [ ] Item 8: Review and categorize all business income and expense transactions
  • [ ] Item 9: Separate personal and business expenses to ensure only eligible deductions are claimed
  • [ ] Item 10: Compile receipts, invoices, and records for all major business purchases over $2,500
  • [ ] Item 11: Calculate and document depreciation for vehicles, equipment, and property using IRS guidelines
  • [ ] Item 12: Verify home office deduction eligibility and measure square footage used regularly and exclusively for business
  • [ ] Item 13: Collect mileage logs and records for business vehicle use, including dates, destinations, and purposes
  • [ ] Item 14: Document business meal and entertainment expenses, ensuring they meet IRS deductibility rules (50% for meals)
  • [ ] Item 15: Review and substantiate travel expenses, including lodging, transportation, and incidental costs
  • [ ] Item 16: Confirm eligibility for the Qualified Business Income (QBI) deduction under Section 199A
  • [ ] Item 17: Identify and document qualified research and development (R&D) tax credits, if applicable
  • [ ] Item 18: Claim the Work Opportunity Tax Credit if you hired employees from targeted groups
  • [ ] Item 19: Verify estimated tax payments made during the year and calculate any remaining balance due
  • [ ] Item 20: Determine if you need to file state and local income, sales, or franchise taxes
  • [ ] Item 21: Prepare payroll tax filings (Form 941) and reconcile wages, withholdings, and deposits
  • [ ] Item 22: Issue 1099-NEC forms to contractors paid $600 or more during the year by January 31
  • [ ] Item 23: Choose the appropriate IRS form (Schedule C, Form 1120-S, Form 1065, etc.) based on business structure
  • [ ] Item 24: Schedule tax filing deadline (April 15 or extended date) and submit Form 4868 for a six-month extension if needed
  • [ ] Item 25: Arrange payment for any tax balance due using EFTPS, credit card, or check to avoid penalties and interest
