40 Remote Work Productivity Checklist

Updated: March 04, 2026

40 checklist items to boost productivity and well-being for remote workers and freelancers.

What's Inside

  • 40 actionable checklist items
  • Covers time management, ergonomics, and mental health
  • Easy to follow daily and weekly tasks
  • [ ] Item 1: Start your day at the same time every morning to build consistency
  • [ ] Item 2: Avoid checking email or messages for the first 30 minutes of your day
  • [ ] Item 3: Drink a glass of water immediately after waking up to rehydrate
  • [ ] Item 4: Complete a 5-minute stretch or light exercise routine to wake up your body
  • [ ] Item 5: Write down your top 3 priorities for the day in a notebook or planner
  • [ ] Item 6: Get fully dressed as if going to an office to shift into work mode
  • [ ] Item 7: Open and organize your workspace with only essential items on your desk
  • [ ] Item 8: Position your monitor at eye level to reduce neck strain
  • [ ] Item 9: Ensure your chair supports your lower back and your feet are flat on the floor
  • [ ] Item 10: Use a wired internet connection or test Wi-Fi speed to confirm reliability
  • [ ] Item 11: Close all non-work-related browser tabs and apps before starting tasks
  • [ ] Item 12: Set up dual monitors or a second screen to reduce window switching
  • [ ] Item 13: Use a task manager (e.g., Todoist, Trello) to log all daily assignments
  • [ ] Item 14: Break large tasks into smaller subtasks with individual deadlines
  • [ ] Item 15: Schedule your most challenging work during your peak energy hours
  • [ ] Item 16: Use time blocking to assign specific hours for focused work and meetings
  • [ ] Item 17: Set a timer for 25-minute work intervals followed by 5-minute breaks (Pomodoro)
  • [ ] Item 18: Turn off non-essential phone and desktop notifications during focus time
  • [ ] Item 19: Define a clear start and end time for your workday
  • [ ] Item 20: Use calendar invites for personal work blocks to protect focus time
  • [ ] Item 21: Respond to messages during designated communication windows, not instantly
  • [ ] Item 22: Use video calls for complex discussions instead of long email threads
  • [ ] Item 23: Confirm meeting agendas are shared in advance for efficient discussions
  • [ ] Item 24: Summarize action items and next steps after every team call
  • [ ] Item 25: Use status indicators (e.g., Slack, Teams) to show availability clearly
  • [ ] Item 26: Overcommunicate project updates to remote teammates proactively
  • [ ] Item 27: Send a daily end-of-day update to your manager or client
  • [ ] Item 28: Take a full lunch break away from your desk to recharge
  • [ ] Item 29: Step outside for at least 10 minutes during midday to get natural light
  • [ ] Item 30: Practice the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds
  • [ ] Item 31: Schedule a 5-minute breathing or mindfulness exercise between tasks
  • [ ] Item 32: Keep healthy snacks within reach to avoid energy crashes
  • [ ] Item 33: Stand or walk during phone or internal meetings when possible
  • [ ] Item 34: Close all work-related apps and browser windows at the end of the day
  • [ ] Item 35: Review your completed tasks and mark them off your list daily
  • [ ] Item 36: Write your top 3 priorities for the next day before logging off
  • [ ] Item 37: Charge your devices overnight in a designated spot away from your bed
  • [ ] Item 38: Limit screen time 30 minutes before bedtime to improve sleep quality
  • [ ] Item 39: Reflect weekly on what improved your productivity and what didn’t
  • [ ] Item 40: Celebrate small wins and completed projects to maintain motivation
