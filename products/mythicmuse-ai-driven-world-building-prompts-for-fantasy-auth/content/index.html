World-Building Prompts

World Foundation Prompts

#### WF-001: Core World Concept

```

Create a comprehensive overview for a fantasy world with the following characteristics: [insert 2-3 key themes or concepts, e.g., "floating islands, crystal-based magic, post-apocalyptic renaissance"]. Include the world's fundamental nature, primary conflicts, and what makes it unique. Provide details about the overall tone, technological level, and how magic or supernatural elements function in daily life. Make this world feel lived-in and authentic.

```

#### WF-002: Cosmology and Realms

```

Design a detailed cosmological structure for a fantasy world that includes [number] different realms or planes of existence. For each realm, describe: its physical properties, inhabitants, how it connects to other realms, and what role it plays in the greater cosmology. Include information about how beings can travel between realms and what barriers or requirements exist for such travel.

```

#### WF-003: Divine Pantheon Generator

```

Create a pantheon of [number] deities for a fantasy world themed around [insert theme, e.g., "elemental forces," "abstract concepts," "natural cycles"]. For each deity, provide: name, domain/portfolio, personality traits, symbols, relationships with other gods, typical worshippers, and how they interact with mortals. Include details about religious conflicts, alliances, and how divine power manifests in the world.

```

Culture & Society Prompts

#### CS-001: Unique Civilization Builder

```

Design a fantasy civilization that has adapted to live in [specific environment, e.g., "underwater cities," "floating mountain peaks," "inside giant trees"]. Detail their: social hierarchy, government structure, daily customs, architectural style, primary occupations, cultural values, coming-of-age rituals, and how their environment has shaped their worldview. Include specific examples of their art, music, and storytelling traditions.

```

#### CS-002: Economic System Designer

```

Create a detailed economic system for a fantasy world where the primary currency is [unusual currency, e.g., "crystallized time," "bottled emotions," "living seeds"]. Explain: how this currency is created/harvested, its exchange rates with other goods, what institutions control it, how different social classes access it, and what happens when the currency is scarce. Include details about trade routes, merchant guilds, and economic conflicts.

```

#### CS-003: Cultural Conflict Generator

```

Develop a complex cultural conflict between two neighboring societies in a fantasy world. Society A values [cultural trait] while Society B prioritizes [opposing cultural trait]. Detail: the historical origins of this conflict, how it affects daily interactions, specific flashpoints that escalate tensions, individuals caught between both cultures, and potential paths to resolution or deeper division. Include cultural practices that highlight these differences.

```

Magic System Prompts

#### MS-001: Unique Magic Framework

```

Design a magic system based on [unique concept, e.g., "manipulating probability," "borrowing abilities from mythical creatures," "weaving sound into reality"]. Define: how magic users discover their abilities, the learning process, physical/mental costs of magic use, societal attitudes toward magic users, limitations and taboos, how magic interacts with technology, and what happens when magic goes wrong. Include specific examples of spells and their effects.

```

#### MS-002: Magic Academy Creator

```

Create a detailed magical institution that specializes in teaching [specific type of magic, e.g., "dream manipulation," "plant communication," "metal singing"]. Include: the school's history and founding, admission requirements, curriculum structure, notable faculty members, student life and traditions, the campus layout and special facilities, graduation requirements, and what careers graduates typically pursue. Add details about school rivalries and unique challenges students face.

```

#### MS-003: Forbidden Magic Explorer

```

Develop a category of forbidden or dangerous magic in your world called [magic type, e.g., "soul weaving," "time fracturing," "reality editing"]. Explain: why this magic is forbidden, who originally practiced it, what catastrophic events led to its banning, secret practitioners who still use it, the temptations it offers, how authorities detect its use, and the consequences for those caught practicing it. Include a specific forbidden technique and its devastating potential.

```

Character Development Prompts

#### CD-001: Complex Protagonist Builder

```

Create a fantasy protagonist who is [profession/role] with the unique ability to [specific power]. This character struggles with [internal conflict] while facing the external challenge of [main plot obstacle]. Detail their: backstory and formative experiences, personality quirks and flaws, relationships with family and friends, moral code and how it's tested, greatest fear and deepest desire, and how their unique ability both helps and hinders them. Include specific scenes that reveal their character.

```

#### CD-002: Morally Ambiguous Antagonist

```

Design a fantasy antagonist whose goals are [seemingly noble objective] but whose methods involve [morally questionable actions]. This character believes they are justified because [their reasoning]. Detail their: tragic backstory that shaped their worldview, loyal followers and why they're devoted, moments of genuine kindness or doubt, the line they won't cross, their relationship with the protagonist, and how they might be redeemed or why redemption is impossible.

```

#### CD-003: Ensemble Cast Generator

```

Create a diverse group of [number] companions for a fantasy adventure, each representing a different [aspect, e.g., "elemental affinity," "social class," "magical school"]. For each character, provide: their role in the group dynamic, unique skills and weaknesses, personal quest or goal, relationships with other group members, comic relief moments, dramatic character arc, and how they grow throughout the story. Include group conflicts and bonding experiences.

```

Formatting and Style Guidance

Optimal Prompt Structure

For best results with AI tools, structure your prompts using this format:

1. Clear Objective: Start with what you want to create

2. Specific Parameters: Include details in brackets for customization

3. Output Requirements: Specify the format and depth of response needed

4. Context Clues: Add relevant background information

Customization Variables

Replace bracketed placeholders with your specific needs:

  • [World Theme]: steampunk, high fantasy, dark fantasy, urban fantasy, etc.
  • [Technology Level]: stone age, medieval, renaissance, industrial, modern, futuristic
  • [Magic Prevalence]: rare and feared, common but regulated, everyday tool, dying art
  • [Tone]: gritty and realistic, heroic and optimistic, mysterious and ethereal, humorous and lighthearted

Response Refinement Techniques

#### Follow-up Prompt Examples:

  • "Expand on the [specific element] you mentioned"
  • "How would this affect [related aspect]?"
  • "What would be the opposing viewpoint to this idea?"
  • "Can you provide more specific examples of [concept]?"
  • "How would a [character type] react to this situation?"

Output Organization

When using these prompts, organize your results into:

1. Core Concepts: Main ideas and themes

2. Detailed Descriptions: Rich, specific information

3. Interconnections: How elements relate to each other

4. Story Hooks: Potential plot points and conflicts

5. Reference Notes: Important details for consistency

Troubleshooting and FAQs

Common Issues and Solutions

#### Q: The AI's response is too generic or clichéd. How can I get more original results?

A: Try these strategies:

  • Add more specific constraints and unusual combinations
  • Ask for "unconventional" or "surprising" approaches
  • Include phrases like "avoid typical fantasy tropes"
  • Request multiple alternatives and combine the best elements
  • Use follow-up prompts to dig deeper into unique aspects

#### Q: The AI generated content that doesn't fit my world's tone. How do I fix this?

A:

  • Be more specific about tone in your prompt (include mood descriptors)
  • Provide examples of the style you want
  • Use follow-up prompts to adjust the tone: "Make this more [serious/humorous/dark/lighthearted]"
  • Reference specific media that captures your desired tone

#### Q: How do I maintain consistency across different prompt sessions?

A:

  • Keep detailed notes on your world's established elements
  • Include key world details in each new prompt as context
  • Create a "world bible" document to reference
  • Use consistent terminology and naming conventions
  • Regularly review and cross-reference new content with existing material

#### Q: The AI's responses are too long/short for my needs.

A:

  • Specify desired length in your prompts ("in 2-3 paragraphs," "provide a brief overview," "give detailed descriptions")
  • Ask for bullet points for concise information
  • Request expansions: "Elaborate on [specific point]"
  • Use "summarize" or "expand" follow-up prompts

#### Q: How can I make AI-generated content more emotionally engaging?

A:

  • Ask for specific emotional impacts on characters
  • Request sensory details and atmospheric descriptions
  • Include relationship dynamics and personal stakes
  • Ask "How would this make someone feel?" as a follow-up
  • Focus on human (or character) experiences within the world elements

Technical Tips

#### Prompt Length Optimization

  • Most AI tools handle 500-1000 word prompts effectively
  • Break complex requests into multiple focused prompts
  • Use bullet points for multiple requirements
  • Save complex prompts as templates for reuse

#### Context Management

  • Remind the AI of key world details when starting new conversations
  • Use consistent character and place names
  • Reference previous outputs when building on established concepts
  • Create "context primer" prompts that establish your world's basics
