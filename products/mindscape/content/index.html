Brainstorming Exercises

Beyond simple prompts, these exercises encourage deeper thinking and collaborative idea generation.

The "SCAMPER" Method (Adaptable for AI)

Instructions: Apply the SCAMPER method to an existing product, service, or content idea. Ask your AI to generate ideas based on each element.

  • Substitute: "How can we substitute elements of [our current blog post format] to make it more engaging? (e.g., substitute text with infographics, substitute long paragraphs with bullet points)"
  • Combine: "What can we combine with [our social media strategy] to create a more impactful campaign? (e.g., combine user-generated content with a live Q&A session)"
  • Adapt: "How can we adapt [a successful marketing campaign from another industry] for our [freelance service business]?"
  • Modify/Magnify/Minify: "How can we modify, magnify, or minify [the length of our podcast episodes] to better suit our audience's listening habits?"
  • Put to Another Use: "What are alternative uses for [the content we create for our blog] that we haven't explored yet? (e.g., repurpose into an ebook, a webinar, or social media graphics)"
  • Eliminate: "What elements can we eliminate from [our current onboarding process] to make it simpler and more efficient for new clients?"
  • Reverse/Rearrange: "How can we reverse or rearrange [the typical order of our service delivery] to offer a unique client experience?"

The "Worst Possible Idea" Exercise

Instructions: Intentionally brainstorm the worst possible ideas. This often sparks creativity by breaking down inhibitions and highlighting what not to do, which can then lead to good ideas by contrast.

  • "Brainstorm the absolute worst possible ideas for a [new social media platform for artists]. What would make it a complete failure?"
  • "Generate the worst possible content for a [blog about personal finance]. What topics or formats would alienate readers?"
  • "Imagine the worst possible product for [small business owners]. What features would be completely useless or detrimental?"

The "Analogy Thinking" Exercise

Instructions: Draw parallels between your problem and a seemingly unrelated field.

  • "What can we learn about [improving customer service for a freelance business] from the principles of [running a successful theme park]?"
  • "How can we apply the concepts of [natural ecosystems] to design a more resilient [online community for creators]?"
  • "If [our content creation process] were a [type of food], what would it be, and how could we make it more delicious and appealing?"

The "What If Your Target Audience Were..." Exercise

Instructions: Imagine your target audience in a different context to uncover new needs and perspectives.

  • "What if your target audience of [busy parents] were actually [astronauts on a space mission]? What new content or services would they need?"
  • "If your ideal client for [a business coaching service] were [a medieval knight], what would their primary challenges and desires be?"
