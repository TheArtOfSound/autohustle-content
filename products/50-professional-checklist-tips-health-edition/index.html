50 Professional Checklist Tips: Health Edition

Updated: March 05, 2026

50 practical checklist items for health professionals.

What's Inside

  • practical tips
  • ready to use
  • expert curated
  • [ ] Item 1: Verify patient's identity and confirm appointment details before starting.
  • [ ] Item 2: Ensure the examination room is clean, well-stocked, and properly equipped.
  • [ ] Item 3: Wash hands thoroughly before and after each patient interaction.
  • [ ] Item 4: Review the patient's medical history and previous records before the appointment.
  • [ ] Item 5: Prepare necessary forms, consent documents, and educational materials.
  • [ ] Item 6: Set up and test all medical equipment and devices to be used.
  • [ ] Item 7: Greet the patient warmly and professionally, introducing yourself and your role.
  • [ ] Item 8: Explain the purpose and process of the appointment to the patient.
  • [ ] Item 9: Ask open-ended questions to encourage patient to share their concerns.
  • [ ] Item 10: Actively listen to the patient, showing empathy and understanding.
  • [ ] Item 11: Document the patient's chief complaint and history of present illness accurately.
  • [ ] Item 12: Perform a thorough physical examination, noting all relevant findings.
  • [ ] Item 13: Order appropriate diagnostic tests or procedures based on clinical findings.
  • [ ] Item 14: Explain the purpose and process of any tests or procedures to the patient.
  • [ ] Item 15: Obtain informed consent for tests, procedures, or treatments as needed.
  • [ ] Item 16: Document all findings, assessments, and plans clearly and concisely.
  • [ ] Item 17: Discuss preliminary findings and initial impressions with the patient.
  • [ ] Item 18: Develop a patient-centered treatment plan, considering their preferences and values.
  • [ ] Item 19: Explain the treatment plan, including benefits, risks, and alternatives.
  • [ ] Item 20: Prescribe medications, if necessary, with clear instructions and warnings.
  • [ ] Item 21: Provide written instructions and educational materials to reinforce verbal advice.
  • [ ] Item 22: Schedule follow-up appointments as needed based on the patient's condition.
  • [ ] Item 23: Coordinate care with other healthcare providers, specialists, or services.
  • [ ] Item 24: Document all follow-up plans, referrals, and consultations in the medical record.
  • [ ] Item 25: Address any patient questions or concerns before the end of the appointment.
  • [ ] Item 26: Summarize key points and next steps for the patient to remember.
  • [ ] Item 27: Ensure the patient understands and agrees with the care plan.
  • [ ] Item 28: Provide contact information for questions or emergencies that may arise.
  • [ ] Item 29: Update the patient's problem list, medications, and allergies in the medical record.
  • [ ] Item 30: Document all relevant details of the appointment, including assessments and plans.
  • [ ] Item 31: Ensure all documentation is complete, accurate, and legible.
  • [ ] Item 32: Review and sign the medical record in a timely manner.
  • [ ] Item 33: Enter any necessary orders or referrals into the electronic system.
  • [ ] Item 34: Communicate relevant information to the patient's primary care provider.
  • [ ] Item 35: Follow up on pending test results, consultations, or other pending items.
  • [ ] Item 36: Address any abnormal or unexpected findings promptly and appropriately.
  • [ ] Item 37: Document any changes in the patient's condition or treatment plan.
  • [ ] Item 38: Ensure all follow-up appointments are scheduled and documented.
  • [ ] Item 39: Review the patient's care plan regularly and update as needed.
  • [ ] Item 40: Monitor and document the patient's progress and response to treatment.
  • [ ] Item 41: Address any patient concerns or questions that arise between appointments.
  • [ ] Item 42: Coordinate care transitions, such as hospital discharge or transfer to another facility.
  • [ ] Item 43: Ensure all necessary paperwork and instructions are provided for care transitions.
  • [ ] Item 44: Follow up with the patient after a care transition to ensure a smooth process.
  • [ ] Item 45: Maintain open and respectful communication with the patient and their family.
  • [ ] Item 46: Advocate for the patient's needs and preferences within the healthcare system.
  • [ ] Item 47: Provide emotional support and reassurance to the patient as needed.
  • [ ] Item 48: Educate the patient about their health condition, treatment options, and self-care strategies.
  • [ ] Item 49: Encourage the patient to ask questions and actively participate in their care.
  • [ ] Item 50: Regularly review and update the patient's care plan based on their evolving needs and preferences.
