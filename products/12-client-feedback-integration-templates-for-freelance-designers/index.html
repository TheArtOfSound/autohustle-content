12 Client Feedback Integration Templates for Freelance Designers

Updated: March 06, 2026

12 ready-to-use templates to help freelance designers collect, organize, and apply client feedback directly into their design workflow.

What's Inside

  • 12 customizable templates
  • structured for clear feedback tracking
  • compatible with Figma and Notion

Template 1: Initial Feedback Request

When to use: After delivering the first draft of a design project, to gather structured input from the client

[Dear [Client Name],

I hope you're doing well! I'm excited to share the initial draft of [Project Name] for your review. This version includes [brief description of key elements, e.g., logo concepts, website layout, brand color palette].

To help me refine the design effectively, I’d appreciate your feedback using the following structure:

  1. Overall Impression: What’s your first reaction to the design?
  2. What Works: Which elements do you like most, and why?
  3. What Doesn’t Work: Are there any aspects that feel off, confusing, or misaligned with your vision?
  4. Specific Requests: Do you have any concrete changes in mind (e.g., color adjustments, layout shifts, font changes)?
  5. Additional Notes: Anything else you’d like me to consider?

Please share your thoughts by [Feedback Deadline], so I can begin revisions promptly. If you'd prefer to discuss this over a quick call, just let me know—I’m happy to schedule a [15/30]-minute check-in.

Looking forward to your insights!

Best regards,

[Your Name]]

Template 3: Revision Round Confirmation

When to use: Before starting revisions, to confirm you’ve correctly interpreted the client’s feedback

[Hi [Client Name],

Thank you for your feedback on [Project Name]. To ensure I’m addressing your needs accurately, I’ve summarized the key changes you requested:

  • [Change #1, e.g., “Shift primary color from blue to green to align with brand refresh”]
  • [Change #2, e.g., “Increase spacing between sections for cleaner layout”]
  • [Change #3, e.g., “Replace stock image with custom illustration direction”]

I’ll begin implementing these updates and aim to deliver the revised version by [Revised Delivery Date].

If I’ve misunderstood anything or if priorities have shifted, please let me know by [Confirmation Deadline] so I can adjust accordingly.

Thanks again for the clear input!

Best,

[Your Name]]

Template 4: Visual Annotation Guide

When to use: When clients struggle to describe design issues in writing—guide them to annotate screenshots directly

[Hi [Client Name],

To help me understand your feedback more precisely, I’ve attached a PDF version of the [Design Page/Screen Name] with clear areas for comment.

How to provide feedback:

  1. Open the PDF in [Adobe Acrobat / Preview / Figma / etc.]
  2. Use the Comment or Sticky Note tool to mark specific areas
  3. For each note, include:
  • What you’re reacting to (e.g., “header font,” “button color”)
  • Your concern or suggestion (e.g., “feels too small,” “should match brand red”)
  1. Save and return the file by [Feedback Due Date]

Pro tip: Circle, arrow, or highlight the exact spot you’re referring to—this saves time and avoids confusion!

Let me know if you need help using the tools.

Thanks,

[Your Name]]

Template 7: Change Request Intake Form

When to use: For formalizing scope changes or new feedback that may impact timeline or budget

[Client Name], thank you for your feedback on [Project Name].

To ensure clarity and alignment, I’ve documented your latest request(s) below:

🔹 Requested Change: [Client’s exact feedback or summarized request]

🔹 Impact on Project:

  • [Timeline: e.g., “Adds 2 business days to revision phase”]
  • [Scope: e.g., “Falls outside original agreed deliverables”]
  • [Budget: e.g., “Requires additional fee of $[Amount]”]

🔹 Options:

  1. Proceed with change: Approve updated timeline and/or budget
  2. Defer change: Include in a future update or Phase 2
  3. Maintain original scope: Continue as planned

Please reply with your choice by [Response Deadline]. I’ll send a revised agreement if needed.

For transparency, here’s our original scope: [Link to SOW or summary].

Let me know how you’d like to proceed!

Best,

[Your Name]
