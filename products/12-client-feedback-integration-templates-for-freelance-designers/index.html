12 Client Feedback Integration Templates for Freelance Designers

Updated: March 06, 2026

12 ready-to-use templates to help freelance designers collect, organize, and apply client feedback directly into their design workflow.

What's Inside

  • 12 customizable templates
  • structured for clear feedback tracking
  • compatible with Figma and Notion

Template 1: Client Feedback Request Form

When to use: After submitting a design concept to the client for review

Thank you for considering my design concept for your [PROJECT NAME]. I would appreciate any feedback you may have to ensure the final product meets your expectations. Please find below a list of aspects I would like you to review and provide comments on:

  • Overall design concept: [PLACEHOLDER]
  • Color scheme: [PLACEHOLDER]
  • Typography: [PLACEHOLDER]
  • Imagery: [PLACEHOLDER]
  • Other (please specify): [PLACEHOLDER]

Your feedback will be invaluable in helping me refine the design. Please return this form by [DEADLINE] to allow sufficient time for revisions.

  • The client will provide timely feedback on design concepts and revisions.
  • The designer will implement revisions based on client feedback within [TIMEFRAME].
  • The number of revisions included in the project scope is [NUMBER OF REVISIONS].

Template 4: Feedback Summary & Action Plan

When to use: After receiving initial feedback, use this to confirm understanding and outline next steps.

Hi [Client Name],

Thank you for sharing your feedback on the [project name/design phase]. I’ve reviewed your comments and summarized them below to ensure we’re aligned on the direction:

Key Feedback Points:

  • [Feedback Point 1]
  • [Feedback Point 2]
  • [Feedback Point 3]

Next Steps I’ll Take:

  • [Action Step 1 – e.g., Adjust color palette to align with brand guidelines]
  • [Action Step 2 – e.g., Reformat layout for better visual hierarchy]
  • [Action Step 3 – e.g., Revise typography for improved readability]

I’ll have the updated version ready by [revised delivery date]. If anything is missing or needs clarification, please let me know by [response deadline] so I can include it in this round.

Thanks again for your clear input—it’s helping shape a stronger final design.

Best,

[Your Name]

Template 5: Clarification Request for Vague Feedback

When to use: When client feedback is unclear or overly broad (e.g., “Make it pop”).

Hi [Client Name],

Thanks for your feedback on the [project name]. I want to make sure I address your vision accurately—could you help me clarify a few points?

You mentioned: “[Exact quote from client, e.g., Make it more modern]”. To refine this effectively, could you:

  • Share 2–3 examples of designs you feel match this style?
  • Let me know which specific element feels “not modern enough” (e.g., fonts, colors, layout)?
  • Clarify if you’d like subtle updates or a bolder redesign?

The more detail you can provide, the better I can align the revisions with your expectations. I’ll hold off on making changes until I hear back so we avoid unnecessary rounds.

Looking forward to your input!

[Your Name]

Template 6: Prioritized Revision Roadmap

When to use: When a client provides extensive feedback and you need to organize and prioritize changes.

Hi [Client Name],

I’ve reviewed your feedback on the [project name] and grouped the requested changes into a clear revision roadmap. To keep things efficient, I’ve prioritized them based on impact and dependencies:

High Priority (Core to function/brand):

  • [Revision 1 – e.g., Update logo to final approved version]
  • [Revision 2 – e.g., Fix alignment issues in mobile view]

Medium Priority (Enhancements):

  • [Revision 3 – e.g., Adjust button colors for better CTA visibility]
  • [Revision 4 – e.g., Refine spacing between sections]

Low Priority (Polish):

  • [Revision 5 – e.g., Change icon style to line icons]
  • [Revision 6 – e.g., Tweak heading font weight]

I’ll begin with high-priority items and check in before moving to lower-priority tweaks. Let me know if you’d like to adjust the priority order.

Best,

[Your Name]

Template 7: Feedback Boundary & Scope Reminder

When to use: When feedback starts to exceed the original project scope.

Hi [Client Name],

Thank you for your ongoing feedback on the [project name]. I’m happy to help refine the design to meet your goals.

As I review your latest requests—including [list major new request, e.g., redesigning the navigation menu and adding animation effects]—I want to gently note that these fall outside the original scope outlined in [Project Agreement/Proposal Name].

To keep the project on track, here are your options:

  • Proceed with minor revisions (included in current scope)
  • Expand the scope with a brief add-on agreement (including time and cost for new requests)

I’m happy to draft a quick scope update if you’d like to move forward with the additional work. Let me know your preference by [date] so I can plan accordingly.

Thanks for understanding!

[Your Name]

Template 8: Round-Based Revision Update

When to use: To maintain structure across multiple feedback rounds and prevent endless edits.

Hi [Client Name],

This is Round [Number] of revisions for the [project name]. I’ve implemented all agreed-upon changes from our last discussion, including:

  • [Change 1 – e.g., Updated primary CTA button to green]
  • [Change 2 – e.g., Centered headline and reduced font size]
  • [Change 3 – e.g., Added client logo to footer]

You can view the updated version here: [Link to design/file]

Please share your feedback by [deadline], focusing only on remaining concerns. As a reminder, our agreement includes [number] rounds of revisions. If additional rounds are needed, they can be added at [rate or package option].

Looking forward to your thoughts!

[Your Name]

Template 9: Visual Annotation Guide Request

When to use: When you need clients to give specific, location-based feedback instead of general comments.

Hi [Client Name],

To help me implement your feedback as accurately as possible, could you please review the [project name] using annotated screenshots?

Here’s how:

  1. Open the design file at [Link to Figma/Adobe XD/Preview]
  2. Use the built-in comment tool to click on specific elements and leave notes (e.g., “Move this logo 10px to the right”)
  3. Hit “Resolve” or “Send” when done

This helps me:

  • Address changes faster
  • Avoid misinterpretation
  • Track exactly what needs updating

I’ll wait for your annotated feedback before starting revisions. Let me know if you need help using the tool!

Thanks,

[Your Name]

Template 10: Feedback Consolidation Follow-Up

When to use: When feedback comes from multiple stakeholders and needs to be unified.

Hi [Client Name],

I noticed that feedback for the [project name] has come from several team members. To ensure we move forward efficiently and avoid conflicting revisions, I recommend consolidating all input into a single, prioritized list.

Could you or your team lead please:

  • Gather all comments from [Team Member 1, Team Member 2, etc.]
  • Confirm which changes are must-haves vs. nice-to-haves
  • Share one unified feedback document by [date]?

This will help me deliver a cohesive update without overlapping or contradictory changes. I’ll hold revisions until I receive the consolidated notes.

Thanks for coordinating!

[Your Name]

Template 11: Post-Feedback Design Rationale Response

When to use: When you disagree with feedback and want to professionally push back with design reasoning.

Hi [Client Name],

Thank you for your feedback on the [project name], especially your note about [specific feedback, e.g., increasing font size for the body text].

I’ve considered this change carefully, and while I understand the intent, I recommend keeping the current size ([X]pt) for the following reasons:

  • It maintains optimal readability at small paragraph widths
  • It ensures visual balance with the heading hierarchy
  • It aligns with accessibility standards (tested at [tool/result])

That said, I’m happy to adjust it to [Client’s Suggested Size] in a test version so you can compare both options side by side. Would you like me to prepare that?

I’m committed to a solution that works for both usability and your vision.

Best,

[Your Name]
