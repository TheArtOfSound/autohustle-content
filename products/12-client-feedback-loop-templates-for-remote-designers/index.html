12 Client Feedback Loop Templates for Remote Designers

Updated: March 06, 2026

12 client feedback loop templates for remote designers — streamline reviews, reduce miscommunication, and get actionable input efficiently.

What's Inside

  • 12 purpose-built templates for feedback collection
  • designed for Figma, email, and async review tools
  • reduces revision rounds by setting clear expectations

Template 1: Initial Project Feedback

When to use: At the beginning of a project to understand client expectations and goals.

Dear [CLIENT NAME],

I hope this message finds you well. I am excited to start working on your [PROJECT NAME] project. To ensure I meet your expectations, I would appreciate your feedback on the following aspects:

  • What are your primary goals for this project?
  • Who is your target audience?
  • What sets your product/service apart from competitors?
  • Are there any specific design preferences (e.g., color schemes, typography) you would like me to follow?

Please feel free to share any additional information that you think would be helpful for me to know. I am looking forward to hearing your thoughts and working together to create an amazing [PROJECT TYPE].

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 2: Design Concept Review

When to use: After presenting initial design concepts to gather feedback and identify the best direction.

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

I hope you like the initial design concepts I shared for your [PROJECT NAME]. I would love to hear your thoughts on the following:

  • Which design concept resonates with you the most, and why?
  • Are there any elements from one concept that you would like to see incorporated into another?
  • Are there any changes or modifications you would like me to make to the designs?
  • Do the designs align with your brand's overall vision and messaging?

Your feedback will help me refine the design and ensure it meets your expectations. Please let me know if you have any questions or need further clarification on any of the concepts.

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 3: Wireframe Feedback

When to use: After sharing low-fidelity wireframes to gather feedback on the layout and functionality.

  • Are there any features or elements that are missing from the wireframes?
  • Do the wireframes meet your accessibility requirements?

Please let me know if you have any questions or need further clarification on any of the wireframes. Your input will help me create a solid foundation for the project.

Best regards,

[YOUR NAME]

Please review these directions and let me know:

  1. Which direction resonates most with your vision
  2. What elements you'd like to see carried forward
  3. Any immediate concerns or adjustments needed

I've included annotations explaining key design decisions. Looking forward to your thoughts by [DATE].

Best,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 3: Specific Feedback Request for Design Review

When to use: When you need structured feedback on specific aspects of a design rather than general impressions

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

To help me iterate effectively on [DESIGN NAME], please focus your feedback on these specific areas:

  1. Visual hierarchy - Is the information prioritization clear?
  2. Color scheme - Does it align with your brand personality?
  3. Navigation flow - Are the user paths intuitive?
  4. Content placement - Is important information easily found?

Rate each area from 1-5 (5 being excellent) and include brief notes about what's working or needs adjustment.

This focused approach will help me make precise improvements. Thanks!

[YOUR NAME]

Template 5: Mid-Project Design Snapshot & Feedback Request

When to use: After completing a key milestone in the design process—such as wireframes, user flows, or initial mockups—and you need structured feedback before moving to the next phase.

Hi [Client Name],

I hope you're doing well! I'm excited to share the latest progress on [Project Name]. We've just wrapped up the [specific phase, e.g., low-fidelity wireframes for the dashboard] and I’d love your input before we move forward.

This snapshot includes:

  • [Brief list of what’s included, e.g., Main user flow screens, Key UI components]
  • [Any relevant context, e.g., Feedback from stakeholder interviews has been incorporated]

🎯 Goal of this feedback round:

To confirm alignment on structure, flow, and core functionality before investing time in high-fidelity visuals.

📌 Please focus your feedback on:

  1. Is the user journey clear and logical?
  2. Are any key features missing or misplaced?
  3. Does the layout support your primary goals for this screen?

🚫 What not to focus on (yet):

  • Final colors, fonts, or imagery (we’ll refine those next)
  • Micro-interactions or animations

📎 Attached: [Link to Figma/InVision/Google Drive file]

🗓️ Please share your thoughts by [Feedback Deadline, e.g., Friday, May 10].

To make it easy, reply directly to this email or leave comments in [design tool name]. Let me know if you'd like a quick 15-minute call to walk through anything!

Looking forward to your thoughts,

[Your Name]

Hi [Client Name],

Thanks again for joining today’s presentation for [Project Name]! I appreciated your time and the conversation we had around the [specific deliverable, e.g., mobile app prototype].

As discussed, here’s a quick summary of the feedback shared during our call:

  • [Summarize key point 1, e.g., “Simplify the onboarding flow by removing step 3”]
  • [Summarize key point 2, e.g., “Move the profile icon to the top-right corner”]
  • [Summarize key point 3, e.g., “Use brand colors earlier in the experience”]

✅ Next Steps:

I’ll incorporate these changes and share an updated version by [Revised Delivery Date].

🔍 Clarification Needed:

  • [Insert any ambiguous feedback, e.g., “You mentioned ‘more modern’—could you share 1–2 examples of designs you consider modern?”]

If I missed anything or you’d like to add notes, please reply with your thoughts by [Follow-Up Deadline, e.g., end of week].

Appreciate your collaboration!

[Your Name]

Template 8: Prototype Usability Feedback Request

When to use: When sharing an interactive prototype and you need feedback focused on usability, flow, and real-world experience.

Hi [Client Name],

I’ve just shared the interactive prototype for [Project Name]—it’s ready for your usability feedback! 🚀

📎 Click here to test it: [Prototype Link]

🎯 Goal: Understand how intuitive the experience feels and identify any friction points.

To simulate a real user:

  1. Complete the primary task: [e.g., “Sign up and schedule a booking”]
  2. Navigate to [Secondary task, e.g., “Update your profile settings”]

📌 Please share thoughts on:

  • Was the flow easy to follow?
  • Did any action feel confusing or hidden?
  • Did the transitions make sense?
  • Any “I expected this to work differently” moments?

💡 Tip: Leave comments directly in [Figma/InVision] or reply with your notes.

🗓️ I’d love your feedback by [Deadline], so we can refine before finalizing.

Let me know if you’d like a quick debrief call afterward!

[Your Name]
