12 Client Feedback Loop Templates for Freelance Designers

Updated: March 05, 2026

12 client feedback loop templates for freelance designers — streamlines review cycles with clear, professional formats for every project stage.

What's Inside

Template 1: Initial Project Brief Feedback

When to use: After sending the initial project brief to the client for review.

Hello [CLIENT NAME],

Thank you for taking the time to review the initial project brief. To ensure we're aligned and ready to move forward, please provide your feedback on the following:

  1. Project Goals:
  • [GOAL 1]
  • [GOAL 2]
  • [GOAL 3]

Are these goals clear and accurate? Please suggest any changes or additions.

  1. Target Audience:
  • [AUDIENCE DESCRIPTION]

Does this accurately represent our target audience? Please provide any additional details or corrections.

  1. Project Scope:
  • [SCOPE ITEM 1]
  • [SCOPE ITEM 2]
  • [SCOPE ITEM 3]

Are there any items you'd like to add, remove, or modify?

  1. Timeline & Milestones:
  • [MILESTONE 1: DATE]
  • [MILESTONE 2: DATE]
  • [MILESTONE 3: DATE]

Please confirm if these dates work for you or suggest any adjustments.

  1. Budget:
  • [BUDGET AMOUNT]

Does this budget align with your expectations? Please let me know if we need to adjust.

Please provide your feedback by [FEEDBACK DEADLINE]. I'm looking forward to your input and starting this project together!

Best,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 2: Concept Approval Request

When to use: After presenting initial design concepts to the client.

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

I've prepared some initial design concepts for [PROJECT NAME] based on our discussions and the project brief. Please review the attached concepts and provide your feedback on the following:

  1. Concept Preferences:
  • Which concepts do you like the most? (Please rank them 1-3)
  • Are there any elements from other concepts you'd like to see incorporated?
  1. Design Elements:
  • Colors: [COLOR PALETTE]
  • Typography: [FONT CHOICES]
  • Imagery: [IMAGE STYLE]

Please share your thoughts on these choices and suggest any changes.

  1. Functionality:
  • [FUNCTIONALITY ITEM 1]
  • [FUNCTIONALITY ITEM 2]
  • [FUNCTIONALITY ITEM 3]

Are there any functional aspects you'd like to add, remove, or modify?

Please provide your feedback by [FEEDBACK DEADLINE]. I'm excited to hear your thoughts and refine these concepts based on your input.

Best,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 3: Revision Request Form

When to use: When asking the client to formalize their revision requests.

Hello [CLIENT NAME],

Thank you for your feedback on the latest design iteration. To ensure we're on the same page and to streamline the revision process, please fill out the following form with your specific requests:

  1. List of Revision Requests:
  • [REQUEST 1: DESCRIPTION & LOCATION]
  • [REQUEST 2: DESCRIPTION & LOCATION]
  • [REQUEST 3: DESCRIPTION & LOCATION]
  1. Priority Level:
  • High: [REQUESTS]
  • Medium: [REQUESTS]
  • Low: [REQUESTS]
  1. Additional Notes or Context:
  • [NOTES]

Please provide as much detail as possible to help me understand and implement your requests accurately. You can also use visual markers (e.g., circles, arrows) on the design files to point out specific areas that need attention.

Please submit your completed form by [FEEDBACK DEADLINE]. I'll review your requests and provide an updated timeline if necessary.

Best,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 8: Final Review & Approval Request

When to use: When seeking client approval on the final design before launch.

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

I'm excited to share the final design for [PROJECT NAME] with you! Please review the attached files and provide your feedback on the following:

  1. Design Elements:
  • Do all the design elements (e.g., colors, typography, imagery) align with your vision for [PROJECT NAME]?
  1. Functionality:
  • Does the design effectively support the intended functionality of [PROJECT NAME]?
  1. Content:
  • Is all the content accurate, up-to-date, and well-presented?
  1. Browser & Device Compatibility:
  • Have you tested the design on different browsers and devices? Please share any issues or concerns.
  1. Final Approval:
  • Are you ready to give the final approval for [PROJECT NAME] to go live?

Please provide your feedback and final approval by [FEEDBACK DEADLINE]. I'm looking forward to your response and launching [PROJECT NAME] together!

Best,

[YOUR NAME]

Template 6: Initial Project Feedback Request

When to use: At the beginning of a project to understand client expectations and goals

[CLIENT NAME], I'm excited to start working on your [PROJECT NAME] project. To ensure I meet your expectations, could you please provide some initial feedback on the following aspects:

  • What are your primary goals for this project?
  • Who is your target audience?
  • What sets your brand apart from competitors?
  • Are there any specific design elements or themes you'd like me to include or avoid?
  • What is the timeline for this project, and are there any critical milestones I should be aware of?

I appreciate your input, and it will help me create a tailored approach for your project.

Template 7: Design Concept Feedback Form

When to use: After presenting initial design concepts to gather specific feedback

Dear [CLIENT NAME],

I've put together some design concepts for your [PROJECT NAME] based on our previous discussions. Please review the attached designs and provide your feedback on the following:

  • Which concept do you like the most, and why?
  • Are there any elements from one concept that you'd like to see incorporated into another?
  • What do you think about the color scheme, typography, and overall aesthetic?
  • Are there any changes or modifications you'd like me to make before proceeding?

Your feedback is invaluable in helping me refine the design to best suit your needs.

Template 8: Mid-Project Check-In

When to use: Halfway through the project to ensure everything is on track and to address any concerns

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

We're halfway through your [PROJECT NAME] project, and I wanted to touch base to ensure everything is meeting your expectations. Could you please let me know:

  • Are you satisfied with the progress so far?
  • Are there any changes in your project goals or requirements that I should be aware of?
  • Have you had a chance to review the latest designs, and do you have any feedback?
  • Is there anything you're concerned about or would like to discuss?

Your input at this stage is crucial for delivering a final product that exceeds your expectations.

Template 9: Revisions Request Form

When to use: When a client requests revisions to the design

Dear [CLIENT NAME],

Thank you for your feedback on the [PROJECT NAME] design. I'm happy to make revisions to ensure you're completely satisfied. To help me understand your requests better, could you please provide detailed feedback on the following:

  • What specific elements of the design would you like me to revise?
  • Could you describe the changes you envision, or provide examples if possible?
  • Are there any new design elements or features you'd like to add?
  • Is there a particular aspect of the design that you feel needs the most attention?

I'm looking forward to working on these revisions and delivering a design that meets your vision.

Template 10: Final Design Approval Request

When to use: When presenting the final design for client approval

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

I'm pleased to present the final design for your [PROJECT NAME] project. I've incorporated all your feedback and revisions to create a design that I believe accurately represents your brand and meets your project goals. Please review the attached final design and let me know:

  • Are you satisfied with the final design, and is there anything else you'd like me to change?
  • Do you approve the design as is, or would you like to request further revisions?
  • Are there any additional assets or materials you need from me to complete your project?
  • Is there a launch date or timeline for implementing the new design that I should be aware of?

Your approval is the final step before considering the project complete.

Template 11: Client Satisfaction Survey

When to use: After completing a project to gauge client satisfaction and gather feedback for improvement

Dear [CLIENT NAME],

Now that your [PROJECT NAME] project is complete, I'd love to hear about your experience working with me. Your feedback is invaluable for helping me improve and provide the best possible service to my clients. Could you please take a moment to answer the following questions:

  • How would you rate your overall satisfaction with the final design and the project process?
  • What did you enjoy most about working with me, and were there any challenging aspects?
  • Were your expectations met, and did you feel supported throughout the project?
  • Do you have any suggestions for how I could improve my services or process?

Thank you for your time, and I look forward to the possibility of working with you again in the future.

Template 12: Testimonial Request

When to use: After a successful project to request a testimonial for marketing purposes

Hi [CLIENT NAME],

I hope you're enjoying your new [PROJECT NAME]! I wanted to reach out because I'm incredibly proud of how the project turned out, and I believe your testimonial could help others understand the value of my services. Would you be willing to provide a brief testimonial about your experience working with me? It could be as simple as:

  • What you liked most about the project process
  • How the final design met or exceeded your expectations
  • Any benefits or results you've seen from the new design

If you're agreeable, I can send over a draft testimonial based on our project discussions for your approval.

Template 13: Follow-Up and Next Steps

When to use: After project completion to check in and discuss potential future collaborations

Dear [CLIENT NAME],

It's been [TIMEFRAME] since we completed your [PROJECT NAME], and I hope you're still enjoying the results. I wanted to check in and see how everything is going. Are there any new projects or initiatives on the horizon that I could discuss, and are there any additional services I could provide to support your ongoing needs? Perhaps we could schedule a call to explore potential next steps or discuss any other questions you might have. I appreciate the opportunity to have worked with you and look forward to the possibility of collaborating again in the future.
