Template 11: Project Kick-off Meeting Agenda

When to use: To structure your initial project meeting with a new client, ensuring all key discussion points are covered and setting clear expectations.

Project Kick-off Meeting Agenda

Date: [Date of Meeting]

Time: [Time of Meeting]

Platform: [Meeting Platform, e.g., Zoom, Google Meet, In-Person]

Attendees:

  • [Your Name/Company Name]
  • [Client Name]
  • [Client Company Name]
  • [Other Key Stakeholders from Client Side]

1. Introductions & Welcome (5 mins)

  • Briefly re-introduce yourselves and your roles.
  • Express enthusiasm for the project.

2. Project Overview & Goals (15 mins)

  • Client to reiterate their understanding of the project brief.
  • Discuss the primary objectives and desired outcomes.
  • What does success look like for this project?

3. Target Audience & Brand Identity (10 mins)

  • Deep dive into the client's target audience.
  • Review existing brand guidelines, voice, and visual style.
  • Discuss any new brand elements to be considered.

4. Scope of Work & Deliverables (15 mins)

  • Confirm the specific services and deliverables for this project.
  • Clarify any assumptions made during the proposal stage.
  • Discuss any potential out-of-scope requests and how they will be handled.

5. Timeline & Milestones (10 mins)

  • Review the proposed project timeline.
  • Identify key milestones and their respective deadlines.
  • Discuss client availability for feedback and approvals.

6. Communication & Feedback Process (10 mins)

  • Establish preferred communication channels ([e.g., email, Slack, project management tool]).
  • Outline the feedback and revision process, including turnaround times.
  • Designate primary points of contact for each party.

7. Questions & Next Steps (5 mins)

  • Open floor for any remaining questions from either party.
  • Confirm immediate next steps (e.g., sending over assets, scheduling follow-up).

Action Items:

  • [List any action items agreed upon during the meeting, with assigned owners and deadlines]

Template 12: Project Brief Questionnaire

When to use: To gather detailed information from your client about their project needs, goals, and preferences before you begin the design process.

Project Brief Questionnaire

Thank you for entrusting [Your Name/Company Name] with your design project! To ensure we fully understand your vision and objectives, please take some time to answer the following questions as thoroughly as possible. This information will be crucial in guiding our design process and delivering a solution that exceeds your expectations.

1. About Your Company/Brand

  • What is the name of your company/brand?
  • What does your company/brand do?
  • What are your company's core values or mission?
  • Who is your primary target audience? (Describe them in detail: demographics, psychographics, needs, pain points)
  • What are your main competitors? (Please list 2-3)
  • What makes your company/brand unique or stand out from competitors?

2. About This Project

  • What is the specific project you need design services for? (e.g., Logo redesign, Website design, Social media graphics, Brochure)
  • What are the primary goals for this project? (e.g., Increase brand awareness, Drive sales, Improve user experience, Launch a new product)
  • What is the desired outcome or feeling you want this design to evoke?
  • Are there any specific features or elements that must be included in the design?
  • Are there any elements or styles that you absolutely want to avoid?
  • Do you have any existing brand guidelines, logos, or assets we should be aware of? (Please attach if possible)
  • What is the primary call to action for this design?

3. Design Preferences & Inspiration

  • Please describe your preferred aesthetic or style. (e.g., Modern, Minimalist, Vintage, Bold, Playful, Professional)
  • Are there any specific colors you'd like to use or avoid?
  • Do you have any examples of designs (competitors or otherwise) that you like or dislike? Please provide links or descriptions and explain why.
  • What kind of tone or personality should the design convey?

4. Logistics & Timeline

  • What is your ideal completion date for this project?
  • Do you have a specific budget allocated for this project? (Optional, but helpful for scope)
  • Who will be the primary point of contact for this project from your end?

5. Anything Else?

  • Is there anything else you think is important for us to know about this project or your business?

Thank you for your time and thoughtful responses! We look forward to working with you.
