40 Essential Tasks for Freelancer Tax Prep

Updated: March 04, 2026

40 clear tasks to help freelancers prepare for tax season.

What's Inside

  • 40 clear tasks
  • organized by priority
  • easy to follow
  • [ ] Item 1: Set a specific date on your calendar for completing your tax preparation
  • [ ] Item 2: Confirm your current legal business structure (sole proprietor, LLC, etc.)
  • [ ] Item 3: Verify your Employer Identification Number (EIN) or Social Security Number is correct on file
  • [ ] Item 4: Open a separate bank account for business income and expenses if you haven’t already
  • [ ] Item 5: Reconcile your business bank and credit card statements for the tax year
  • [ ] Item 6: Download and install reliable accounting software or update your existing one
  • [ ] Item 7: Back up all financial records and store them securely in the cloud or on an external drive
  • [ ] Item 8: Create a master folder (digital or physical) to store all tax-related documents
  • [ ] Item 9: Gather all 1099-NEC forms received from clients for the tax year
  • [ ] Item 10: Request copies of missing 1099-NEC forms from clients who haven’t sent them
  • [ ] Item 11: Collect all 1099-K forms from payment processors like PayPal, Stripe, or Square
  • [ ] Item 12: Download annual transaction summaries from all freelance platforms used (e.g., Upwork, Fiverr)
  • [ ] Item 13: Compile records of all business income, including direct bank deposits and cash payments
  • [ ] Item 14: Print or export monthly profit-and-loss statements from your accounting software
  • [ ] Item 15: Collect receipts and records for all office equipment purchases over $100
  • [ ] Item 16: Gather receipts for home office expenses, including utilities and internet if applicable
  • [ ] Item 17: Calculate your home office deduction using either the simplified or actual expense method
  • [ ] Item 18: Track mileage for business-related travel using a log or app
  • [ ] Item 19: Total all mileage reimbursements or deductions for vehicle use
  • [ ] Item 20: Collect receipts for business meals (50% deductible) with notes on purpose and attendees
  • [ ] Item 21: Compile subscription costs for business software, apps, and tools (e.g., Canva, Zoom)
  • [ ] Item 22: Gather invoices and receipts for professional services (accountant, lawyer, coach)
  • [ ] Item 23: List all education expenses related to improving freelance skills or services
  • [ ] Item 24: Identify and total contributions made to retirement accounts (e.g., SEP IRA, Solo 401(k))
  • [ ] Item 25: Review health insurance premiums paid and determine eligibility for self-employed deduction
  • [ ] Item 26: Identify any eligible tax credits, such as the Qualified Business Income Deduction (Section 199A)
  • [ ] Item 27: Reconcile estimated tax payments made during the year using Form 1040-ES records
  • [ ] Item 28: Calculate any underpayment or overpayment of estimated taxes
  • [ ] Item 29: Determine if you need to make a final estimated tax payment before year-end
  • [ ] Item 30: Organize documents in the order they appear on IRS Form 1040 and Schedule C
  • [ ] Item 31: Choose a tax preparation method: CPA, tax software, or DIY with IRS Free File
  • [ ] Item 32: Schedule an appointment with a tax professional specializing in self-employment taxes
  • [ ] Item 33: Enter all income and expenses into tax preparation software or provide them to your accountant
  • [ ] Item 34: Double-check your Social Security Number and business EIN for accuracy on all forms
  • [ ] Item 35: Review your filing status and update dependents or credits if needed
  • [ ] Item 36: Complete and verify accuracy of Schedule C (Profit or Loss from Business)
  • [ ] Item 37: Complete Schedule SE (Self-Employment Tax) to calculate Social Security and Medicare owed
  • [ ] Item 38: E-file your federal tax return or print and mail it with all required forms and attachments
  • [ ] Item 39: Pay any remaining tax balance by the April deadline to avoid penalties and interest
  • [ ] Item 40: Save a complete copy of your filed return and all supporting documents for at least three years
