50 Checklist Items for Small Business Tax Preparation

Updated: March 05, 2026

50 checklist items for small business tax preparation — covers deductions, records, and deadlines.

What's Inside

  • 50 comprehensive checklist items
  • organized by tax category
  • easy-to-follow format
  • [ ] Item 1: Gather and verify your Employer Identification Number (EIN) or Social Security Number (SSN) for tax filing
  • [ ] Item 2: Confirm your business legal structure (sole proprietorship, LLC, S-Corp, etc.) for correct form selection
  • [ ] Item 3: Verify your business name and address are up to date with the IRS
  • [ ] Item 4: Confirm your business activity code (NAICS) is accurate for your primary services
  • [ ] Item 5: Ensure your business registration and licenses are current with state and local agencies
  • [ ] Item 6: Collect all Form 1099-NEC from clients for non-employee compensation received
  • [ ] Item 7: Gather Form 1099-K statements from payment processors (PayPal, Stripe, etc.)
  • [ ] Item 8: Compile year-end bank and credit card statements for all business accounts
  • [ ] Item 9: Reconcile your accounting software (e.g., QuickBooks) with bank records
  • [ ] Item 10: Review profit and loss (P&L) statement for accuracy and completeness
  • [ ] Item 11: Verify your balance sheet reflects accurate asset, liability, and equity values
  • [ ] Item 12: Confirm all business income is recorded, including cash, digital payments, and barter transactions
  • [ ] Item 13: Identify and document any forgiven PPP loan amounts and supporting forgiveness documentation
  • [ ] Item 14: Review and categorize all business expenses by type (e.g., office, travel, advertising)
  • [ ] Item 15: Collect receipts or invoices for all business-related purchases over $75
  • [ ] Item 16: Separate personal and business expenses in all accounts and records
  • [ ] Item 17: Verify home office space meets IRS eligibility (regular and exclusive use)
  • [ ] Item 18: Calculate home office deduction using the simplified or regular method
  • [ ] Item 19: Document mileage logs for business use of personal vehicle (date, destination, purpose, miles)
  • [ ] Item 20: Calculate vehicle expense deduction using IRS standard mileage rate or actual costs
  • [ ] Item 21: Review and substantiate all travel expense claims (lodging, meals, transportation)
  • [ ] Item 22: Confirm meals deduction complies with current IRS rules (50% or 100% if eligible)
  • [ ] Item 23: Compile receipts for business-related education and training courses
  • [ ] Item 24: Document subscriptions to professional journals, software, or cloud services
  • [ ] Item 25: List all office supplies and equipment purchases with purchase dates and costs
  • [ ] Item 26: Identify assets purchased over $2,500 to evaluate Section 179 expensing or depreciation
  • [ ] Item 27: Confirm health insurance premiums paid as a self-employed individual are documented
  • [ ] Item 28: Verify retirement plan contributions (SEP-IRA, Solo 401(k)) were made and recorded
  • [ ] Item 29: Calculate and document deductible portion of cell phone and internet expenses
  • [ ] Item 30: Review and claim eligible research and development (R&D) tax credits
  • [ ] Item 31: Check eligibility for Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC) if applicable
  • [ ] Item 32: Confirm estimated tax payments made during the year and record dates and amounts
  • [ ] Item 33: Prepare Form 941 for quarterly federal tax returns (if you have employees)
  • [ ] Item 34: Reconcile payroll tax liabilities with deposits made via EFTPS
  • [ ] Item 35: Verify Form W-2s are accurate for all employees and provide copies by January 31
  • [ ] Item 36: Issue Form 1099-NEC to all independent contractors paid $600 or more
  • [ ] Item 37: Submit Form 940 for annual Federal Unemployment (FUTA) tax
  • [ ] Item 38: Confirm state unemployment (SUTA) tax payments and filings are up to date
  • [ ] Item 39: Review local business tax or privilege license requirements and deadlines
  • [ ] Item 40: Verify sales tax collected is accurately reported and remitted to state authorities
  • [ ] Item 41: Obtain and review K-1 forms from partnerships or S-corporations if applicable
  • [ ] Item 42: Confirm inventory records are accurate if your business sells products
  • [ ] Item 43: Evaluate eligibility for Qualified Business Income (QBI) deduction under Section 199A
  • [ ] Item 44: Double-check all bank account and routing numbers for tax refund or payment setups
  • [ ] Item 45: Choose and sign designation on Form 8879 if e-filing with a preparer
  • [ ] Item 46: Back up all tax records and supporting documents electronically and physically
  • [ ] Item 47: Set a calendar reminder for next quarter’s estimated tax payment deadline
  • [ ] Item 48: Schedule time to meet with your tax professional before the filing deadline
  • [ ] Item 49: Submit federal income tax return (Form 1040 with Schedule C, Form 1120, etc.) by April 15
  • [ ] Item 50: Mail or e-file state income tax return by the state-specific deadline
