40 Checklist Items for Freelancer Tax Preparation

Updated: March 05, 2026

40 checklist items for freelancers to prepare for tax season — covers deductions, income, and documentation.

What's Inside

  • 40 checklist items
  • organized by tax category
  • clear instructions
  • [ ] Item 1: Gather all 1099-NEC forms received from clients for the tax year
  • [ ] Item 2: Collect 1099-K forms from payment processors like PayPal or Stripe
  • [ ] Item 3: Compile records of all freelance income, including cash and international payments
  • [ ] Item 4: Reconcile total income reported on tax forms with personal financial records
  • [ ] Item 5: Separate business income from personal income in your accounting system
  • [ ] Item 6: Review bank and credit card statements for unrecorded freelance deposits
  • [ ] Item 7: Verify that all client payments are accounted for in your books
  • [ ] Item 8: Calculate gross freelance income for the tax year
  • [ ] Item 9: Identify all business-related expenses incurred during the year
  • [ ] Item 10: Collect receipts and invoices for office supplies and equipment
  • [ ] Item 11: Document home office dimensions and calculate deductible square footage
  • [ ] Item 12: Determine whether to use the standard or actual expense method for home office deduction
  • [ ] Item 13: Track mileage for business-related travel using a log or app
  • [ ] Item 14: Collect toll and parking receipts for business trips
  • [ ] Item 15: Compile costs of software subscriptions used for freelance work
  • [ ] Item 16: Record expenses for domain names, website hosting, and online portfolios
  • [ ] Item 17: Gather receipts for professional development courses or certifications
  • [ ] Item 18: List fees paid to accountants, lawyers, or tax professionals
  • [ ] Item 19: Document costs of business insurance premiums (e.g., liability, E&O)
  • [ ] Item 20: Add up subscription costs for productivity or project management tools
  • [ ] Item 21: Itemize cell phone and internet expenses used for business purposes
  • [ ] Item 22: Allocate a percentage of personal phone and internet bills to business use
  • [ ] Item 23: Record expenses for business meals with clients (50% deductible)
  • [ ] Item 24: Verify that business meal receipts include date, amount, purpose, and attendees
  • [ ] Item 25: Compile costs of marketing, advertising, and freelance platform fees
  • [ ] Item 26: Document travel expenses for client meetings or industry conferences
  • [ ] Item 27: Save boarding passes, hotel invoices, and rental car receipts for business trips
  • [ ] Item 28: Calculate depreciation for computers, cameras, or other equipment over $2,500
  • [ ] Item 29: Claim the Qualified Business Income (QBI) deduction if eligible
  • [ ] Item 30: Determine eligibility for the 20% pass-through deduction under Section 199A
  • [ ] Item 31: Check if you qualify for the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) as a low-income freelancer
  • [ ] Item 32: Verify eligibility for the Self-Employed Health Insurance Deduction
  • [ ] Item 33: Sum up contributions to a SEP-IRA or Solo 401(k) for deductible retirement savings
  • [ ] Item 34: Calculate estimated tax payments made during the year (Q1–Q4)
  • [ ] Item 35: Reconcile quarterly estimated tax payments with IRS Form 1040-ES records
  • [ ] Item 36: Obtain an Employer Identification Number (EIN) if operating as an LLC or hiring contractors
  • [ ] Item 37: Issue 1099-NEC forms to subcontractors paid $600 or more during the year
  • [ ] Item 38: Verify that all 1099 recipients’ names and TINs match IRS records (via W-9 forms)
  • [ ] Item 39: Choose a tax filing method (e.g., self-prep, software, CPA) and schedule submission
  • [ ] Item 40: Set a reminder to file Form 1040 and Schedule C (and SE if applicable) by the April deadline
