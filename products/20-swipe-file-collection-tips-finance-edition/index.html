5. Investment Portfolio Diversification Strategy

Use case: When advising clients on diversifying their investment portfolios

Diversification is key to managing risk and maximizing returns in any investment portfolio. A well-diversified portfolio should include a mix of assets such as stocks, bonds, real estate, and commodities. The allocation between these assets should be based on the investor's risk tolerance, investment horizon, and financial goals.

Example Allocation:

  • Stocks: 40%
  • Bonds: 30%
  • Real Estate: 15%
  • Commodities: 10%
  • Cash: 5%

Regular portfolio rebalancing is essential to maintain the desired asset allocation and ensure the portfolio remains aligned with the investor's objectives.
